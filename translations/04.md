# Chapter 4: Li Yecheng

"Uncle, spare my life!"

The man was terrified out of his wits. The moment he hit the ground, he began to beg for mercy, shrinking into a ball as he clung to Li Mutian.

"Ah! Spare me, Uncle, spare me, Uncle, spare my life..."

Li Yecheng had only wanted to steal a melon to satisfy his craving; he never imagined that, in the blink of an eye, the uncle he feared most would be standing right in front of him, terrifying him so much he could not move. Who could have known that Li Mutian would still be holding a long blade in one hand, further shattering his courage.

"Yecheng?" Li Mutian focused his gaze and frowned deeply, deeply, a dark, shifting storm of emotions crossing his face.. Stroking the beard on his chin, he stared at him intently.

Li Yecheng was the older brother of Li Yesheng; their father had been bedridden with illness for years, yet the elder Li Yecheng idled away his days, roaming about to scrounge off others.

The two youths also hurried over upon hearing the noise. Li Tongya raised his long staff and pressed it directly onto Li Yecheng's arm, terrifying him into a sobbing, snot-nosed mess. Li Changhu lifted his head to take a look and could not help but be moved.

"Cousin?" Li Changhu let out a long breath and asked in confusion.

"Why are you here?" Li Tongya asked coldly, the force of the long staff in his hands undiminished.

"He came to steal our melons!" Seeing Li Yecheng stammering and hesitating, Li Mutian answered for him with a cold face. Retracting his long blade, he turned his head and walked away.

"Cousin, pardon the offense."

Li Tongya also withdrew his force and lifted his staff. Tossing down a single sentence, he followed his father back into the house. Only Li Changhu remained behind. He helped Li Yesheng up and wiped the dirt from his face. After speaking a few gentle words of comfort, he politely escorted him out of the courtyard.

---

Inside the house.

Li Chejing and Li Xiangping sat blankly by the table. With the mirror hidden in his robes, Li Xiangping dared not move a muscle. Fortunately, it was not long before his father and second brother walked into the main hall.

"Where is Eldest Brother?"

He looked behind them and hurriedly asked.

"Seeing the guest off."

Li Mutian shook his head.

"I know that child Yecheng often comes to scrounge off others, but I am terrified that word of tonight's events will leak out and reduce our entire family to ashes."

Li Tongya found a small stool and sat down. Hearing this, he tilted his head to look at Li Xiangping and was just about to speak.

"Creak."

The main door let out a sound as it closed. Eldest Brother Li Changhu, with a puzzled expression, sat down on a stool and spoke to their father, Li Mutian.

"Father, was this truly necessary?"

He shook his head.

"Yecheng was merely stealing a single melon. Instead of doing him an effortless favor, you insisted on offending his family."

"What 'his family'? There is only one Li family. I am the primary branch, and they are the collateral."

Li Mutian leaned against the window, listening intently. He waved to his wife and youngest son. "The two of you, go squat by the front and back doors. If anyone comes, call out."

The two voiced their compliance and withdrew.

Li Mutian turned back and tightly sealed the doors and windows. He patted Li Xiangping on the shoulder and said, 'Speak!'

Li Xiangping nodded vigorously and spoke in a tiny voice, "Today, I went to the Meichi River to catch fish, and I picked up this from the river."

Having spoken, he looked at his father Li Mutian. Seeing him nod, he pulled the mirror out from his robes.

Li Changhu looked at his younger brother, then at his father. Taking the blue-grey mirror, he scrutinized it carefully but could not discern anything special about it.

Li Mutian took the mirror from his eldest son's hands. Finding the small hole in the roof, he moved a stone stool, placed the mirror directly beneath it, and narrowed his eyes at his two sons.

They watched as the moonlight rippled ceaselessly like water, gathering above the mirror's surface like young swallows returning to the forest, manifesting a faint white lunar halo that resembled a gemstone.

Li Changhu shot up from his chair, staring fixedly at the lunar halo on the mirror. Li Tongya also lost his composure and was visibly moved, gazing thoughtfully at the mirror.

For the duration it takes to drink a cup of tea, the Li family members silently stared at the mirror without uttering a single word, their expressions varying.

This was already Li Xiangping's second time seeing this scene, yet he was still shocked pale, murmuring to himself, "In all my life, I have never seen such a beautiful sight..."

"Haha, never mind you, even your old man has never seen such a fine thing."

Li Mutian chuckled lowly, yet there was no trace of a smile; instead, his gaze was ruthless.

"This is likely the possession of an immortal."

Li Tongya spoke quietly, picking up his long blade and constantly wiping it. He stared at the mirror, appearing calm, but his incessantly trembling hands betrayed his inner feelings.

"If word leaks out, it will also bring the calamity of clan extermination upon our family." Li Changhu paced continuously around the main hall, his expression both anxious and excited.

"If this is a lost item of an immortal, and they perform an art to seek it out tomorrow, how shall we handle ourselves?" Li Tongya stared intently at his father, speaking with no small amount of worry.

Li Xiangping nodded, lowering his brows and saying in a muffled voice, "Whatever the immortal might bestow upon us as a reward would be boundlessly beneficial for our family."

"Nonsense." Li Mutian waved his hand, "I have heard that there are many bloodthirsty ones among the immortals. We absolutely must not! Who knows how long this mirror had been at the bottom of the water? If they wanted to retrieve it, they would have picked it up long ago. How would it ever fall to us mortals? I see it as highly likely that the immortal has already met with an untimely end."

These wildly rebellious words left Li Changhu trembling with fear; he frowned, not knowing what to think.

Li Tongya, however, suddenly realized the truth, speaking in a low, deep voice, "But was it seen by that brat Li Yecheng?"

"I showed this object to Father in front of the house. At that time, Brother Yecheng should have been in the fields." Li Xiangping lowered his eyes.

"I will go kill him!"

Li Tongya's expression was ruthless. Without hesitation, he threw on the rattan armor, picked up his blade, and headed for the door. It was the first time Li Xiangping had ever seen such a look on his brother's face, and he could not help but cast a few extra glances.

"Come back!" Li Mutian frowned and stopped Li Tongya, casting a glance at Li Xiangping that left him looking flustered.

"Father!" Li Tongya finally grew anxious, speaking with a hardened tone.

"This Li Yesheng is fickle and lacking in loyalty, excessively arrogant, yet he bites the hand that feeds him. Rather than letting a slip of his tongue leak the news and draw the calamity of clan extermination to our Li family, it is better to let him die first."

_It seems Second Brother has learned a great deal from the village teacher,_ Li Xiangping thought silently, lowering his head in shame. Back when their father had sent the three of them to study, both of his older brothers had won the teacher's favor. Only he had been young, ignorant, and mischievous, and now he actually could not understand the idioms in his brother's words.

"Then if your youngest brother were to bite the hand that feeds him and fail to keep his mouth shut, would you kill him as well?" Li Mutian laughed heartily and asked his second son in return.

"Our family does not have such cowards." Li Tongya said indifferently.

Li Mutian shook his head. Raising a hand, he shifted the wooden table back into place, leaped into the air, tapped lightly on the table, and pulled a wooden box down from the roof beams.

Placing the wooden box on the table, he spoke solemnly to the three brothers before him, "There are some things you all should know. I left the village at thirteen, following the Guli Road into Anli County. General Yang received imperial orders to attack Mount Yue and was conscripting soldiers along the Guli Road. At that time, your father had nowhere to shelter himself, so I had no choice but to join the army."

"General Yang disciplined his troops strictly, yet he ate and lived alongside us, close as family. He passed down the Yue Troop Martial Arts to us, instructing us to train our martial arts well. These martial arts are widely circulated; they are easy to learn but difficult to master. Even if an ordinary person succeeds in training them, they will merely become agile and vigorous, without showing any miraculous aspects." Li Mutian sighed.

Seeing that the others were listening very seriously, he continued, "After returning to the village, I learned to read and write. I transcribed the martial arts onto this wooden slip from memory, and I have already had you all practice it carefully. To this day, it has yet to produce any notable results. This is the first thing."

Li Mutian opened the wooden box. Lying inside were a wooden slip, a talisman, and a few miscellaneous items like broken silver and glazed glass.

---

**Translator's Note**

Yes, you guessed it right. The "Li" in Guli Road and Anli County is the same "Li" as Great Li Mountains.

If we translated Guli Road (古黎道), it would be "Ancient Li" Road.

And for Anli County (安黎县), it would be "Peace for the Li" County.
