# Chapter 5: The Rarity of Immortal Destiny.

"Back when we fought the Mount Yue, there were many shamans among them who drove miasma, snakes, and insects. The military distributed these talismans to ward off their shamanic arts. This talisman saved my life. Now, its divine light no longer manifests, and it is just like a piece of torn paper. This is the second thing."

"The Mount Yue were fond of wearing beast bones, feathers, and glazed glass. I killed men and scavenged these valuable objects from their corpses. This is the third thing."

"Tomorrow, I will take this piece of glazed glass and tell your Second Uncle that this is what Xiangping picked up from the river. Whether Li Yecheng saw it or not, we will insist firmly that it is just a piece of broken glazed glass that merely sparkled under the moonlight." Li Mutian took out a piece of broken glazed glass, packed away the other items, and said softly to Li Tongya.

"Father's considerations are thorough." Li Changhu nodded. "Second Uncle is a generous man who understands the greater picture and values the grand scheme. He will not bicker over this."

"It is a pity that Li Yecheng is a piece of trash," Li Tongya said softly, turning his head to look at his father.

Li Mutian picked up the mirror, placed it on the wooden table, and shook his head.

"Now we must see how this treasure is to be used, and in what manner. If not, it may well be like drawing water with a bamboo basket[^1]—all in vain."

Lu Jiangxian had been listening to everything throughout the journey back from within the mirror, and a plan formed in his mind. He was unable to move within the mirror. Whether to protect himself or eventually escape this mirror, if he wished to forge a traversable path upon the long and boundless road of immortality, he would inevitably have to rely on the people and resources of this land.

The Li family came from a background of peasant farmers with shallow foundations, yet surprisingly, several of them stood out far above the norm.

The father, Li Mutian, possessed excellent courage and insight. The eldest son, Li Changhu, was tactful in his affairs and generous in nature. The second son, Li Tongya, was brave, decisive, and daring to fight. Li Xiangping and Li Chejing were also sharp and nimble individuals. Truly, they were an ideal family to align himself with.

What was more, he had neither hands nor legs; he could neither walk nor run. To say nothing of finding replacements, was he to remain silent and be thrown back into the river as trash, suffering a century of silence like imprisonment?

_No matter what, I must first go look at the north, even if it is only a distant glance._ Thinking of this, Lu Jiangxian urged the currents of qi within his body to surge toward the top-left corner of the mirror's body.

Inside the house, the Li brothers surrounded the mirror and tinkered with it for half the day to no avail. They were extremely cautious, terrified of damaging it and losing their immortal destiny. The lunar essence felt as cool and comfortable as water to the touch, yet it could neither be blown away nor drawn in.

It was not until Li Xiangping picked up the mirror and gently stroked its surface that Lu Jiangxian channeled his qi, causing the top-left corner of the mirror to abruptly light up. Startled, Li Xiangping trembled and cried out repeatedly, not knowing whether to hold it or set it down.

Li Tongya moved closer for a look. A bright white arc of light had lit up on the top-left corner of the mirror's body; it was thin at the edges and thick in the middle, looking extraordinarily beautiful. Only after a few breaths did it slowly dim.

"Brother, it lit up!" Li Xiangping said excitedly, suppressing his voice.

Li Tongya took the mirror and, imitating Li Xiangping, stroked its surface. Sure enough, a beam of white light flared. He handed the mirror to their father, signaling for him to do the same.

Both Li Mutian and Li Changhu stroked the mirror, clicking their tongues in praise.

Li Tongya, however, let out a soft exclamation of surprise. Taking the mirror back from his eldest brother, he turned his back to the others and gave it a stroke.

"Father, no matter where this object is stroked from, the faint light that flares always points towards the north. It is just like..." Li Tongya spoke solemnly, gazing at the mirror in his hands.

"A compass." Li Mutian nodded.

Within the mirror, Lu Jiangxian could not help but applaud loudly. _This kid is truly clever; he understands with just a single hint!_

"We go to the village entrance first."

Li Mutian stroked his beard, then turned and went back into the house, calling out to the boys.

"Fetch a string of cured meat. We are going to pay a visit to the teacher."

---

Wiping his eyes, Li Yecheng entered his front courtyard, cursing and swearing as he kicked the pebbles on the path.

"Treating a few melons like treasures, those cowardly turtles coming out with blades in the middle of the night—those idiots must be hiding something!" Sitting on a stone, a thought stirred in his heart."

"Li Mutian went out and messed around for twenty-some years; he must have quite a few good things on him! Of course there are treasures." He quietly calculated in his mind.

"It just so happens that those turtles are also incredibly stubborn, too stingy to let even a speck of dirt slip through their fingers. That old man is old and obstinate, refusing to speak even to the death. Otherwise, once Li Mutian dies, we would all have a share of his things!"

Li Yecheng tilted his head and looked at the courtyard gate as his younger brother, Li Yesheng, timidly entered.

Yecheng's eyes widened in a glare, and he cursed loudly, "You brat, get over here!"

He grabbed his younger brother's clothes and hurled him toward the front of the house. Li Yesheng rolled miserably on the ground, shrinking into a ball in fear.

"I heard you are on good terms with that dog Li Xiangping. Tomorrow, you go to his house and steal two melons for us to eat," he cursed viciously.

---

Li Mutian led the three boys from the end of the village to the front. Along the way, villagers resting against their door thresholds greeted them with smiles.

"Uncle Mutian! Where are you heading off to?"

"Delivering some things to the teacher!"

Li Mutian smiled and lifted the cured meat in his hands.

Arriving at the village entrance, Li Mutian looked around, patted the shoulders of Li Tongya and Li Xiangping, and said in a low voice, "Go."

The two understood his intent. Bending over, they rapidly vanished into the dense reed marshes.

Li Mutian gazed worriedly into the distance. His eldest son could not help but remind him, "You should have instructed them earlier: if there is anything miraculous, they should only observe from afar and retreat. They must not linger."

"Tongya has a sense of measure."

Li Mutian narrowed his eyes, all his calculations flashing through his mind like fleeting shadows.

He understood that tonight, he might lose two of his children, yet he would still have Li Changhu as his heir. However, the children might also return safely, bringing an opportunity for the Li family to soar to great heights. He felt profound, agonizing guilt, clenching his fists tightly.

"Two hundred years."

The Li family had lived upon this land with their faces to the yellow earth and their backs to the sky for exactly two hundred years. Faced with a potential fortuitous encounter, Li Mutian had rationally made a choice aligned with the clan's interests, yet emotionally, he was in endless torment.

"Brother Li!"

The courtyard gate abruptly opened, interrupting Li Mutian's thoughts. The teacher, Han Wenxu, holding a small bowl, stared at him blankly.

"Teacher."

A hearty smile appeared on Li Mutian's face. He strode over to the table and set the cured meat down upon it.

"How could I accept this." Han Wenxu smiled and nodded.

He took the cured meat, snapped off a piece, and sliced it finely onto a small plate. He also stir-fried some pickled vegetables and brought them out on another small plate. The two men moved a small desk over, poured two small cups of rice wine, and sat by the door chatting.

"It seems those immortals who fly high back and forth in the sky are finally not coming anymore." Li Mutian let out a long sigh, revealing a relaxed expression.

Han Wenxu shrugged his shoulders, then replied with evident envy, clicking his tongue, "Immortals!"

"The year I was twelve, an immortal came to the town." He pursed his lips and spoke melancholically, "He said he was looking for individuals whose apertures had opened. Out of the more than a thousand young children in the entire town, he only found three with opened apertures, and he took those three away. Today, among those immortals soaring through the heavens, there might very well be my townsfolk."

"An immortal destiny is a rare thing indeed!" Li Mutian remained silent for a long time before comforting him.

"A rare thing... an immortal destiny is truly a rare thing..."

Han Wenxu stared blankly at the moon in the sky, continuously murmuring these words under his breath.

Both men harbored their own thoughts, falling into a heavily worried silence.

[^1]: A traditional allegorical saying. Because a woven bamboo basket is full of holes, using it to fetch water is impossible. It serves as a metaphor for a completely futile endeavor where all effort ultimately amounts to nothing.
