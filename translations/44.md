# Chapter 44: New Spring

The snow fell for three days, silver-gray flakes rampaging through the air until every household barred their doors. The weather turned thoroughly cold. Li Tongya tidied the small courtyard in Lijing, leaving it in the care of Li Qiuyang, before leading Liu Rouxuan up Mount Lijing.

Trudging unevenly through the deep snow, Liu Rouxuan was draped in a pale gray fur cloak. She gripped Li Tongya's hand with a cheerful smile, her voice chirping like a bird.

"Brother Tongya, let us wed once spring arrives."

"Very well."

Li Tongya brushed the snow from her shoulder. With a light smile, he tilted his umbrella slightly to shake off the accumulated flakes, then leaned it closer to Liu Rouxuan to shield her.

Mount Lijing was not tall. Li Tongya guided Liu Rouxuan through the mist clinging to the base of the mountain, walking along the snow-covered stone path until they reached the front of the courtyard.

Upon entering the house, they found Tian Yun huddled in a fur wrap, sitting by the hearth with a beaming smile. Her cheeks were slightly flushed from the heat.

Seeing the two enter, she called out, "Second Brother, Second Sister-in-law."

Li Tongya gave a warm acknowledgment. Liu Rouxuan, facing this sister-in-law who was five or six years her senior, acted with natural grace. She huddled by the fire, giggling as she began a whispered conversation with Tian Yun.

Li Xiangping had gone down early that morning to cast spirit rain over the rice fields and was currently in the back courtyard, absorbing qi and cultivating. Li Tongya unfastened his heavy coat, hung it on the wall, and turned to look at Lady Ren, who was quietly sewing nearby.

"Where is Father?" he asked softly.

"The elderly sleep more in the winter; he is still in his room," Lady Ren replied, looking up with a smile. Seeing Tian Yun waving from the hearth, she set aside her needlework and joined them, beginning to question Liu Rouxuan with a gentle, low voice.

Li Mutian had personally worked to widen the skylight the day before, busy until late into the night, and was still asleep.

Li Tongya stared blankly out the window at the snow covering the entire mountain, a thought echoing in his heart, _If only Eldest Brother were still here... how wonderful that would be._

---

Winter faded and spring arrived. The previous year had been prosperous; the spring rains were fine without drought, and the autumn was dry without floods. There were no hailstorms nor locust plagues.

The land tax remained at a steady ten percent, leaving the villagers far better off than in previous years. With every household possessing surplus grain, not a single soul had frozen to death that winter.

The second batch of spirit rice planted in Lijing the year before had also matured. Once harvested and husked, it yielded one hundred and seventy catties of spirit rice and fifty catties of spirit rice bran. Combined with the earlier harvest, they now possessed two hundred and ninety catties of spirit rice and ninety catties of spirit rice bran—more than enough to cover the two hundred catties required for their tribute.

With the help of Li Qiuyang and Liu Rouxuan, Li Tongya and Li Xiangping found their burdens much lightened. Entrusting the numerous spirit fields to the younger pair allowed the brothers to focus more of their energy on cultivation.

The White Essence Fruit trees had also blossomed, their branches covered in a flurry of white and pink. Li Tongya estimated that the fruit would be ripe in a few months, ensuring they would not miss the day the Azure Pond Sect emissary came to collect the tribute.

Li Tongya had just finished casting spirit rain when he saw Wan Tiancang entering the courtyard with a look of utter misery.

He immediately began to pour out his grievances, "That Ji Dengqi crossed the border under the cover of night yesterday, masked and intent on raiding our Wan family spirit fields. Nearly ninety percent of the spirit rice at the foot of the mountain was burned, and many mortals were killed. He even nearly fought his way up Mount Huaqian."

"Fortunately, our Patriarch was vigilant during the harvest season and personally presided over the array. Ji Dengqi led a group in a siege throughout the night and only retreated sullenly at dawn."

Li Tongya set down his teacup, his voice tinged with surprise as he remarked, "Your clan's array formation is truly formidable to hold out against a Qi Refinement cultivator's siege for an entire night."

Wan Tiancang waved a hand dismissively and smiled bitterly.

"It was merely a clever trick. Two hundred years ago, our Wan family produced a master of arrays who constructed that great array by harnessing a spiritual spring on Mount Huaqian. As long as the spring flows, the array can continuously replenish its spiritual qi."

"What a magnificent array!" Li Tongya could not help but praise.

He thought to himself, _The array on our own Mount Lijing is far inferior by comparison. However, Xiao Yuansi once said that if we found a better array, we could still use those array flags to set it up! It is a pity our family lacks an inheritance in the Dao of Arrays..._

Wan Tiancang cast a subtle glance at Li Tongya. Seeing him lost in thought, he continued, "Unfortunately, that ancestor was arrogant and tried to establish his Dao Foundation too hastily, only to perish on Mount Huaqian."

"Establish a Dao Foundation?" Li Tongya felt a pang of regret and said deeply, "He must have been a senior at the peak of Qi Refinement."

"Sigh..." Wan Tiancang let out a long sigh, his voice filled with yearning. "That was the most glorious era for my Wan family. We had no fewer than five Qi Refinement cultivators. Yet within a dozen years of that ancestor’s death, the family lost three painstakingly managed peaks. Four Qi Refinement and ten Fetal Breathing cultivators died in battle. If not for our connections within the sect, our family would have been wiped out long ago."

"Later, the elders passed away one by one. The one remaining in the sect only cared to protect the Wan family bloodline and no longer interfered in our affairs. Each subsequent generation became weaker than the last, but we managed to survive. It was only a decade ago, when our Patriarch broke through to the Jade Capital Wheel, that he could finally open several storage bags passed down from our ancestors—only to find nothing left but a few spirit stones and talismans, providing no real help."

"I see," Li Tongya sighed deeply. He refilled the teacup before Wan Tiancang and offered a few low words of comfort as he watched the man lament.

Wan Tiancang took a sip of tea and suddenly slammed the table in fury.

"I loathe that Ji family! Back then, when our Wan family was locked in constant war with the Yu family to the north, the Ji family was nothing but a tiny clan caught in the middle, without even a single Qi Refinement cultivator. Now, by playing both sides, they have grown to such an extent that they have nearly pushed us to a dead end."

Looking at Li Tongya, Wan Tiancang asked in a low voice, "I wonder about that Great Lord in your household?"

Li Tongya knew Wan Tiancang was referring to Li Mutian and immediately grew wary. Fortunately, the brothers had long since coordinated their story.

Taking a sip of tea, he said, "My father left home in his youth to serve as a soldier on Guli Road, following General Yang in the campaign against Mount Yue."

"I have heard a little of this. That Yang Tianya had a Foundation Establishment cultivation and led his troops east to strike that branch of Mount Yue. Many people died." Wan Tiancang shifted his teacup, asking curiously, "But I heard that when your father returned, he was but a mortal..."

Li Tongya smiled bitterly and explained, "My father received guidance from an immortal cultivator at home and attained Qi Refinement. He later found an opportunity during his military service to establish his Immortal Foundation. As for why his cultivation was wasted, that is not a matter for outsiders."

"It was my indiscretion!" Wan Tiancang cupped his hands. He thought to himself, _He likely established his Immortal Foundation at a young age and offended some scion of a great clan in his youthful arrogance. Only those from great clans would be unafraid of retaliation and take pleasure in the drama of crippling someone back into a mortal._

Aloud, he said with regret, "What a pity, what a pity."

Seeing Li Tongya remain silent, Wan Tiancang gritted his teeth and finally spoke his true purpose.

"To be honest with you, Brother Li, I have come this time hoping to trade our clan's array inheritance for a talisman capable of slaying Ji Dengqi—something at the peak of Qi Refinement or even Foundation Establishment!"

The realization dawned on Li Tongya. He thought to himself, _I was wondering why Wan Tiancang came all this way to chat about family history. This fellow had this scheme in mind all along!_
