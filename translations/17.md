# Chapter 17: Ill-Fated Karma (II)

Seeing the fierce and ruthless expression plastered across Li Chejing's still-immature face, Ye Changfu, standing among the crowd, paused.

Holding his torch and stroking his chin, he pondered, _I fear this fourth son is no easy character to deal with either. The leading gentle deer is dead, leaving behind three vicious wolves. The elder is cunning, and the younger is ferocious. Within ten years, Lijing Village will surely become the absolute domain of the Li family!_

The crowd fell silent for a few breaths before someone answered in a low voice, "Changhu was murdered by a refugee!"

Holding back his tears, Li Xiangping knelt beside Li Changhu. He turned his head to look at Tian Shoushui, choking back a sob as he asked, "Uncle Tian, what happened?"

"It was most likely that remnant of the Yuan family," Li Mutian squeezed the words through his teeth. Looking at Tian Shoushui, who had his head bowed in ashamed silence, Li Mutian glanced at the surrounding villagers and called out, "Tongya!"

Hearing this, Li Tongya wiped away his tears. He stepped forward, cupped his hands toward the villagers, and said loudly, "We have alarmed everyone tonight. Please disperse for now; there is no need to linger here."

After speaking, he bowed to help Liu Linfeng up, whispering into his ear, "Uncle, please take your men and detain those refugees first, lest anyone use the chaos to cause further trouble. We will follow shortly."

"Good, good..."

Kneeling before Li Mutian, Liu Linfeng had already been cowering, feeling as if needles were pricking his back. Hearing these words, he felt as though he had received a grand pardon. Nodding repeatedly, he led his men away.

Tian Shoushui and Ren Ping'an carried Li Changhu's body from the ground and laid him in the Li family's rear courtyard. Cries suddenly erupted from the main courtyard; Ren Ping'er had fainted upon hearing the tragic news. Tian Yun and Lady Liu fought back their tears—one stayed to care for her, while the other hurried to fetch the doctor.

Tian Shoushui set Li Changhu down, tears welling in his eyes. "Eldest Brother..."

Just as he was about to speak, Li Mutian waved his hand and said wearily, "Ping'an, go check on Ping'er. Shoushui, go watch the refugees with Liu Linfeng for now. Without my Li family declaring our stance, I fear his hands are tied."

"Yes." Tian Shoushui froze for a moment, then wiped his tears, assented, and withdrew. Ren Ping'an merely nodded blankly and went to look after Ren Ping'er.

With no outsiders left in the rear courtyard, only the Li brothers remained, weeping softly.

Li Mutian could finally control himself no longer. Like a lone, wounded wolf, he sat beside his eldest son and let out a low, mournful wail.

"Changhu—"

The brothers also began to cry. Li Tongya and Li Xiangping managed to maintain some restraint, but the young Li Chejing wept as if his heart were being torn out.

---

In the dead of night, Lijing Village was plunged into chaos. Old Man Xu, leaning on his hoe for support, walked alone to the rear mountain. Twisting and turning along the narrow path through overgrown weeds, a sprawling cluster of tombs soon appeared before his eyes.

Old Man Xu halted and peered closely. Sure enough, a young man in tattered clothes with an animal hide draped around his waist was sitting leisurely beside a small tombstone in the corner. His legs were spread wide as he muttered to himself.

His ears twitching, the youth acutely raised his head to look. Seeing that the newcomer was merely an honest-looking old farmer who appeared too frail to even walk, he actually clapped his hands.

Tilting his head with a smile, he said, "Where does this old man come from!"

Old Man Xu ignored him. He deliberately walked at a snail's pace, trembling as he shuffled forward. Feigning vigilance, he shot the youth a glance before kneeling beside a tomb. Hugging the tombstone, he began to weep and complain in a low voice.

The youth listened carefully and vaguely made out words like "a great grievance avenged" and "rest in peace, master." His heart immediately skipped a beat.

His family had been ruined when he was but a child, forcing him into a life of wandering and destitution. He had spent the first half of his life working as a tenant for others, clutching his dagger every night and practicing bitterly. His sole desire was to one day personally sever Li Mutian's head to offer as a sacrifice to his parents.

Now that his great vengeance was partially fulfilled, his heart brimmed with extreme elation. He had a thousand words he wished to speak, yet there was no one beside him to share in his joy and satisfaction. Why not converse with this old man, and then simply kill him before leaving? Would that not be the best of both worlds?

At this thought, the youth smiled and said, "You come to weep at the Yuan family's tombs. Are you not afraid the Li family will bring you trouble?"

"This old man's days are numbered..."

Old Man Xu replied, wiping away his tears. He cast a low glance at the youth, suddenly turned pale with fright, and threw himself to the ground, crying out, "Young Master!"

"Hm?"

The youth was startled, thinking to himself, _Could this old man have seen me at the village entrance? Better to just kill him and flee, to save myself the trouble._

Old Man Xu, however, covered his tearful face and continued, "The mistress used to carry the young master to sit by the fields, which is why I recognize you. This old man still remembers the three black moles on your foot as proof."

Having lived for over seventy years, Old Man Xu was as cunning as an old fox. By forcefully reversing cause and effect, he shocked the youth so much that his killing intent dissipated.

Stunned, the youth hurriedly asked, "Then do you remember my mother's face?"

"Naturally, I remember."

Old Man Xu pulled a branch from a haystack. Using his hoe to loosen the soil, he began to draw carefully.

The youth was endlessly excited, yet he repeatedly lowered his head to stare gloomily at Old Man Xu. Thoughts constantly flashed through his mind; one moment he felt he should just kill him, and the next he thought he should kidnap him to ask for more news of his mother.

Before long, Old Man Xu finished his drawing and stood up. Though he was but a farmer, he had spent years painting door gods and auspicious symbols for the villagers. Over time, he had acquired some skill in painting, and with just a few strokes, he had actually sketched the subject's spirit to life.

"Mother!"

The youth dropped to his knees to look. Seeing those faint, dream-like features, he immediately began to sob softly. Twenty-two years of grievance and suppression entirely surged into his heart, and his tears flowed like a spring.

Old Man Xu stood to the side, sighing sorrowfully and incessantly praising the mistress's goodness. For a time, the youth could not stem his tears at all.

"This old man still remembers the master's appearance. Allow me to draw it."

Hearing Old Man Xu's words, the youth already believed him eight parts out of ten. Wiping away his tears, he agreed without even raising his head.

Old Man Xu raised his hoe to loosen the dirt for the drawing, a ruthless gleam flashing in his eyes. The hoe was lifted high, its bright silver blade catching a fleeting glint of moonlight, before plunging down violently—smashing straight toward the youth's neck.

The youth had trekked with the refugees all day, expended his utmost effort to assassinate Li Changhu, and then thrown off Tian Shoushui in the reed marshes. He was already exhausted to the point of collapse. Now, struck by overwhelming joy and profound sorrow, his legs had long since gone soft. Having neither the presence of mind nor the means to evade, he was struck dead-on by Old Man Xu.

Old Man Xu farmed year-round, so his body was still robust. This smash landed with a sickening thud, enough to snap tendons and shatter bones. The youth pitched forward into the dirt, his head lolling unnaturally. His eyes rolled back, his limbs convulsed uncontrollably, and white foam frothed from his mouth.

Capitalizing on his fall, the old man brought the hoe down a second time, striking the youth squarely in the head and extinguishing his life entirely.

Still uneasy, Old Man Xu wildly smashed down a dozen more times, sending blood and flesh flying, until white and red spilled across the ground. Turning the youth over, he saw that the breath had left him, though his corpse still bore a look of sorrow on its face.

Only then did his strength finally leave him. Plonking down onto the ground, he covered his face with his withered hands and began to wail in agony, "Ill-fated karma! Ill-fated karma..."
