# Chapter 13: Murder

Li Yecheng's father died last year. As the eldest son, all of the family's land and property naturally fell to him. Feeling absolutely delighted, he quickly sold off a plot of land for some money and feasted to his heart's content at the small shop in the village.

Since then, he had idled away his days, wandering aimlessly through the village and the mountains. He grew increasingly brazen in his thievery and harassment. In all of Lijing Village, aside from his eldest uncle Li Mutian, there was not a single person he feared or respected.

The truly pitiful one was his younger brother, Li Yesheng. Though the exact same age as Li Xiangping, while Xiangping had studied with the teacher at the edge of the village since childhood, Li Yesheng was left utterly alone, suffering from hunger and cold.

Barely a teenager, he had just lost his father and was forced to herd ducks and watch over cattle for others every single day. Were it not for the frequent assistance from Li Mutian's family, he would have likely starved to death in his own home long ago.

Li Yecheng could not care less whether his brother lived or died. Whenever he had free time, an itch would rise in his heart. Watching Li Changhu's family go in and out of their grand compound built of blue bricks drove him mad with jealousy.

"We both carry the Li surname. What gives you the right to be the legitimate branch while I am a concubine's son? What gives you the right to buy fertile land and build a high courtyard! You are just a wealthy family in a village; what right do you have to put on airs and build a grand compound? As if you have some sort of treasure hidden inside!"

He suddenly stood up straight, a sudden realization hitting him as he recalled a night from a few years ago—the tense expressions of his relatives and the blade in Li Mutian's hand.

_Li Mutian does have a treasure..._

Chewing on a piece of dogstail grass, Li Yecheng watched Li Changhu chatting and laughing with the tenant farmers in the fields, a dark sense of resentment brewing in his heart.

_It is nothing more than being born into the right womb._

After a short nap in the shade of a tree, Li Yecheng noticed the night growing deep. He spat out the dogstail grass with a "pah," rolled up his pant legs, and stealthily crept toward the Li family compound.

Circling the perimeter of the courtyard, Li Yecheng could not find a single crack in the wall. He clawed at the wall a few times, but it was built perfectly flat and smooth, making it incredibly difficult to scale.

"Dog bastards."

Li Yecheng spat on the wall, a surge of ruthlessness rising within him as he sprinted toward the rear mountain.

"I refuse to believe you can put a dome over your entire roof."

The Great Li Mountains meandered and twisted continuously. Lijing Village's rear mountain was just one of its peaks; the villagers could not be bothered to name it, so they simply called it the rear mountain.

Crossing the rear mountain and heading south led to Meichi Peak, and continuing south down that mountain led to Jingyang Village. The two villages traded goods via the small paths that snaked through the mountains.

Pushing through the thickets, Li Yecheng climbed along the path for half a quarter of an hour. He found a protruding spot in the mountain with an open, sweeping view, where he crouched low and peered down at the small courtyard below.

He rubbed his eyes. He could hazily see several figures moving about, seemingly sitting cross-legged. One figure was carrying a piece of bluestone, walking back and forth, doing who knows what.

"I heard these bastards placed stone locks in their courtyard. Could it be that Li Mutian acquired some secret martial arts while out in the world and is secretly teaching it to those little bastards?"

Li Yecheng looked up at the sky. It was already gloomy in the mountains, and with dark clouds obscuring the bright moon, the earth was pitch black. The figures below gradually became impossible to discern.

The night was late. The high-pitched cries of apes and the howling of jackals and wolves echoed through the mountains. A chilling wind blew straight up Li Yecheng's pant legs, causing him to clamp his legs together and fall onto a large stone on his rear.

_How eerie._

Li Yecheng sat there for another half a quarter of an hour, shivering so hard that clear snot ran freely from his nose. In his mind, he silently drafted the rumors he planned to spread throughout the village.

_What treasure passed down from our ancestors? I, Li Yecheng, am also of the Li family—why did I not get a share? If it is a martial art, I could not care less about cultivating it; selling it for money would let me live comfortably for many days._

Looking down at the courtyard again, not a single figure remained. Li Yecheng paid it no mind. Tightening his cuffs, he stood up and prepared to head home.

"Mother of God!"

Who could have known that the moment he turned his head, he would see a figure standing quietly on the mountain path. Scared out of his wits, Li Yecheng tumbled and crawled, practically rolling back behind the large bluestone.

Trembling as he peeked his head out, he saw that the figure possessed refined features and was looking down at him. It was none other than his cousin, Li Xiangping.

Li Yecheng widened his eyes, assuming a belligerent posture. Just as he was about to launch into a tirade, he saw Li Xiangping form a hand seal.

"What?"

A dazzling golden gleam flared before his eyes, followed by a sharp, agonizing pain in his throat. The world spun wildly; one moment he saw the bright, luminous moon, and the next, the hazy little courtyard bathed in moonlight.

Li Yecheng's head was severed, landing heavily on the mountain path. His lingering consciousness gazed in confusion at the cold, icy smile on Li Xiangping's face. He only felt that this cousin was more unfamiliar than he had ever been.

Behind the stone, the neck of the headless corpse was spurting blood with a sickening sound. The steaming hot blood stained the bluestone, the dirt path, and the dead leaves red, flowing all the way to Li Xiangping's feet.

Taking a step back in disgust, Li Xiangping watched Li Yecheng's corpse collapse. He pondered for a moment, then cast the art once more, summoning a golden gleam.

Crouching down, he sliced the corpse into large segments with wet, tearing sounds, ensuring they were of suitable proportion so that the wild beasts drawn by the scent of blood would carry them to different parts of the mountain.

Having finished this, Li Xiangping stood up and scanned his surroundings. The shadows between the trees already held numerous pairs of gleaming green eyes.

Clapping his hands together, Li Xiangping said softly, "Take your time dining, gentlemen."

---

The Li family.

Li Changhu awoke from his meditation to find his two younger brothers missing. The massive rear courtyard was so quiet that only the chirping of insects could be heard. Filled with doubt, he hurriedly stepped into the main courtyard, where he finally saw Li Tongya.

Looking at Li Tongya, who was sitting at the wooden table leafing through an incantation, Li Changhu asked with a hint of confusion, "Why do I not see Xiangping?"

"He went to wash his hands."

Li Tongya unhurriedly rolled up the wooden slips and looked at his eldest brother.

"Tongya, have you begun preparing to condense the Profound Scenery Wheel?" Li Changhu pressed his hands onto the wooden table, asking with a trace of envy.

"In a few days, I will have condensed eighty-one wisps of lunar essence. We are far inferior to Chejing; I always feel the need to wait a little longer and prepare for a few more days."

Li Tongya smiled. He picked up a small strip of white cloth, wrapped it tightly around the wooden slips several times, and tied a slipknot.

"Eldest Brother, among the four of us, who do you think is most like Father?" Li Tongya asked suddenly, staring intently at Li Changhu.

"You, naturally," Li Changhu blurted out, then paused. Finding a seat, he smiled and continued, "I am too lenient, Xiangping is too restless, and Chejing is too bashful. Only you, Tongya, remain calm and collected, betraying nothing of your thoughts. You are the most like him."

"Hahaha." Li Tongya let out a dry laugh. Waving his hand, his expression turned serious as he said, "Eldest Brother, do not flatter me. In my view, among the four of us brothers, Xiangping is the most like him."

"Why?" Li Changhu looked at his brother in confusion.

Li Tongya spoke slowly, "When we were little, playing in the courtyard, Father once said: The first time one kills, they may experience ringing in the ears and a feverish head, advancing and retreating without measure; or they may tremble unceasingly, unable to control themselves; or they may roar endlessly, forgetting heaven and earth entirely."

"Only he, Li Mutian, after killing his first man, sheathed his blade, drank his wine, and chatted and laughed as if nothing had happened. After saying this, he laughed heartily, exceedingly proud of himself."

"Xiangping is the most like Father."

Li Tongya lowered his voice, leaning close to Li Changhu's ear, and whispered softly, "Because he is sufficiently cruel."
