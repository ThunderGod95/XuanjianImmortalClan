# Chapter 2: The Li Family

Li Mutian woke up in the early hours of the morning. He lay awake, staring at the dilapidated roof of his house, where a faint glimmer of light seeped through the pitch-black darkness.

A few days ago, a small hole had broken open in the roof, but Li Mutian had no time to mend it. He had not slept well for three entire days. Looking at his wife sleeping heavily beside him, Li Mutian let out a deep sigh.

_Women know nothing of the true gravity of things. These past few days, those lofty immortals have been flying madly back and forth through the Great Li Mountains, seemingly wanting to dig three feet into the earth. They terrified everyone; every time a streak of light flew past, folks would kneel and kowtow..._

Li Mutian furrowed his brows tightly, his heart filled with anxiety. The few small villages nestled at the foot of the Great Li Mountains had always been carefree, but these days they were on edge, everyone fearing for their own safety.

_Deep in the mountains, the paths are narrow; the imperial court cannot govern us, and we do not care for their governance. But when these immortals fight, a single immortal technique could strike Lijing Village so completely that not even a dog would survive._

Li Mutian tossed and turned, unable to fall back asleep. He got up and gazed out the window into the thick darkness of the night.

_The boys are growing bigger by the day, eating more with every meal. Tomorrow, I will send one to the Meichi River to catch some fish and crabs._

_If one day we are struck to death by an immortal technique, that is simply fate. The Li family has been reclaiming and tilling this wasteland for over two hundred years; our roots are too deep to be moved._ Li Mutian lowered his eyes and shook his head. Clasping his hands behind his back, he walked out the door.

The big yellow dog outside the door was still sleeping soundly. Li Mutian walked unhurriedly through the thin morning mist, watching Lijing Village awaken from its slumber amid the crowing of roosters, the barking of dogs, and curling wisps of hearth smoke.

"Xiangping!" Li Mutian shouted at the top of his lungs toward the house. A clattering and crashing resounded from within, and as the main door opened, a half-grown boy dashed out, grumbling.

"Father!" Li Xiangping's features were rather clear and handsome, though his eyes held a sly glint.

He tilted his head back to look at Li Mutian and asked, "What work are we doing today?"

"Go to the Meichi River and fetch some fish and crabs," Li Mutian waved his hand, "There is no heavy labor today. Go get some fresh flavors for your mother to eat."

"Great!"

Li Xiangping nodded repeatedly in excitement. Picking up a woven rope basket and a long pronged fork, he scurried off in a single breath.

Li Mutian chuckled twice and headed toward the fields.

The Meichi River was both shallow and wide, its banks flanked by large stretches of mudflats and reeds. The geese and ducks raised by the village in their tens and hundreds required no feeding; they were let out early in the morning and would all head into the river.

Later in the evening, someone from the village would make their way to the riverbank and call out to the birds. Those geese and ducks were domesticated, accustomed to the voice, and would waddle their way back home.

When Li Xiangping arrived, the flocks had not yet been released. The Meichi River was deserted, with only two small wooden rafts bobbing by the shore. He rolled up his pant legs and sleeves, knelt into the silt, and blindly felt around in front of him with both hands. Fixing his gaze upon the water, he caught sight of a bluish-green tail.

"A fine fish."

Li Xiangping pressed down forcefully and took a deep breath, plunging headfirst into the water. His right hand tightened, and he pulled up the green-tailed fish by its gills.

"Hehe."

Li Xiangping let out a few loud laughs and tossed the fish into his basket. The fish in the Meichi River were definitely never this foolish; this green-tail was, in all likelihood, a wild fish that had darted down from upstream, allowing this child to get lucky.

Looking toward the soles of his feet, a trace of doubt seemed to appear in Li Xiangping's expression. There appeared to be a spot on the riverbed that was excessively smooth, faintly revealing a silver gleam.

Just as he prepared to hold his breath and dive back in to investigate, a loud shout rang out from the shore:

"Brother Xiangping!"

Li Xiangping subconsciously hid the basket behind his back and looked toward the shore, only to see a child of about ten years old pushing his way out from the reeds.

"Oh, it's Little Brother Ye! Are you here to herd the ducks?"

"Mm!"

His younger cousin, Li Yesheng, nodded obediently and smiled.

"I heard a strange tale early this morning. A massive stag was bitten on the foot by a snake and died at the village entrance. Its antlers were the size of a desk, truly a terrifying sight."

Listening to his idle chatter, Li Xiangping merely breathed a sigh of relief and offered the basket forward.

"Look at this green-tail, caught it with my bare hands."

"A fine fish!" Li Yesheng looked down into the basket and smiled with envy.

Li Yesheng's father was bedridden year-round with illness, and his elder brother was an idle layabout. They often ate one meal without knowing where the next would come from. On ordinary days, he had no choice but to go beg for food at the home of his eldest uncle, Li Mutian. His older cousin, Li Xiangping, had always treated him like a blood-brother.

After chatting for a few more sentences, Li Yesheng shook his head and said, "Alright, brother, I must go back and watch the ducks. If even two go missing, my brother will surely beat me to death."

"Go on, go on."

Li Xiangping was also anxious to look at the object at the riverbed, and hurriedly shooed him away.

"Alright!"

The moment his front foot left, Li Xiangping held his breath and dove headlong to the riverbed. He groped around back and forth, and actually managed to feel out a round object.

"Pfft."

Li Xiangping wiped his face and took a look at the object in his hands.

This thing was about the size of a palm, with an ash-cyan, small circular plate at its center, hooped around the edge by what seemed to be a dark iron rim. There was nothing extraordinary to be seen about it.

The front was shattered into seven or eight fragments, barely held together by the rim so as not to fall apart. The back, however, bore a rather bizarre symbol, which Li Xiangping stared at for a long while without being able to discern what it resembled.

_It does look a bit like aunt's mirror._ Li Xiangping's maternal aunt had the largest fields, and only she could afford to use a bronze mirror; ordinary village girls made do with looking at their reflections in the water. When his aunt first obtained that mirror, his mother even brought him to see it. It was indeed more convenient than water.

But this mirror in his hands was far too blurry, completely fogged over so that nothing could be seen. He shook his head in pity, tossed the object into his basket, and turned back to continue catching fish.

---

Lu Jiangxian had already been in the water for nearly half a month. Since the third day, the aura of the lunar essence had stagnated and ceased to increase. He had worked hard for another week, but the result was not the slightest bit of progress. Aside from making himself emit light, he could do absolutely nothing.

Early this morning, just as he was staring blankly at a large green fish beside him, a large hand directly pressed the fish into the silt. The surroundings shook, and the hand grabbed the fish by the gills and lifted it away.

Lu Jiangxian was still caught in the complex emotions of seeing a living person for the first time, when he saw a large hand directly scoop up his true body.

Seeing a rather handsome large face, Lu Jiangxian grew somewhat nervous for a moment. He then saw the other party babble a few words before backhanding him into a basket, leaving him staring eye to eye with the green-tail.

In a flash, Lu Jiangxian also realized a grave problem: he could hear, but he could not necessarily understand.

The local dialect sounded somewhat like the dialects of the Min and Zhe regions from his past life. He truly could not understand a single word. This meant that even if he could produce sound, the other party would completely fail to understand his words. This would serve as a massive hindrance to his assimilation into this world.

Watching small fish being tossed into the basket one after another, Lu Jiangxian condensed his qi and calmed his spirit to perceive the surrounding situation.

Watching the boy carefully raise the wooden fork, Lu Jiangxian let out a soft exclamation of surprise. Under this perspective, he could vaguely perceive the boy's mental activities, sensing roughly which fish at the riverbed his attention was focused upon.

With the aid of this probing sense, and by listening to the boy mutter to himself every time he caught a fish, it was not long before Lu Jiangxian grasped the specific pronunciations for the numbers three to six, as well as the various breeds of fish.

_I will take it one step at a time._

Watching the boy stand up and leave, Lu Jiangxian sighed. This child looked like a farmer's son; he would likely first hand over this bronze-like object to his parents.

He needed to come into more contact with people, see if he could slowly learn the local dialect, and, while preserving himself, find a method to elevate the power of his lunar essence.

**Translator's Note: The Two "Li"s**

You might notice that the Li family lives right next to the Great Li Mountains and Lijing Village. It makes it sound like they own the entire region, right?

It is actually just a quirk of translation! In the original Chinese text, these are two completely different characters. The "Li" used for the family name (李) literally translates to "plum" and is one of the most common surnames in China. Meanwhile, the "Li" int he geographic names (黎) translates to "dawn" or "the multitude."

So, while they look identical in English, there is no historical or ancestral connection between the family and the land they live on. The Li family simply happens to reside at the foot of the Great Li Mountains!
