# Chapter 30: Discussion

Having extracted every bit of information Wan Yuankai knew, Li Xiangping finally saw off the despondent Wan Yuankai. Carefully putting away the rough map in his hands, he took Li Yesheng and returned to Lijing.

Thanks to the continuous stream of field taxes delivered year after year from the various villages, the Li family was able to support more artisans. These people primarily built the small courtyards on the rear mountain, and when the construction schedule was loose, they were also responsible for repairing Lijing.

After more than half a year of renovations, Lijing had changed its appearance. Polished bluestone slabs were pieced together one by one, extending from the Li family's front door all the way to the village entrance. As the main artery of Lijing, it broadcasted influence for this hub of power over the surrounding villages.

The wealthy households in the village also began to imitate the Li family's blue-brick and tiled houses, remodeling their own adobe and thatched cottages. Liu Linfeng and the others did not dare to use blue bricks, opting instead for the slightly inferior white bricks, and then using plaster to outline the wall seams. Although not as striking or beautiful as blue bricks with white seams, it could still be considered grand.

Looking from afar toward the clusters of buildings behind the village, the white-brick courtyards surrounded the blue-brick and gray-tiled main residence. Amidst the ethereal wind and rain, it carried the flavor of a southern river town[^1].

As Li Xiangping returned to the courtyard, he saw Li Mutian resting there with Li Xuanxuan. Li Mutian was full of vigor and agile in his movements. Holding a tea bowl in his hand, he sat calmly at the table, seemingly having already walked out from the pain of losing his son years ago.

Nowadays, Li Mutian did not manage many affairs, but no one dared to underestimate this old man. Although Li Mutian was merely a mortal, Li Yesheng and the others feared him far more than they did Li Tongya or Li Xiangping.

"Xuan'er!"

Li Xiangping smiled and caught the lunging Li Xuanxuan in his arms. The child had reached the age for schooling, and the Li family had hired Han Wenxu to be his primary teacher. Li Xuanxuan was bright and clever, making him effortless to teach.

Li Xuanxuan giggled, hugging Li Xiangping as he spoke in a childish voice, "Today, Xiewen and I went to the reed marshes. He said that Uncle Yesheng used to herd ducks there when he was little!"

The Li family had taken charge of arranging a marriage for Li Yesheng. Li Xiewen was Li Yesheng's child, just over two years old, who ran around after Li Xuanxuan every day.

"Isn't that the truth." Behind them, Li Yesheng answered with a smile.

Li Xiangping turned his head and instructed, "Call Second Brother back."

 He watched Li Yesheng acknowledge the order and retreat, gradually walking away, before looking back at Li Xuanxuan.

"At that time, your Third Uncle was on the side catching fish," Li Xiangping said, setting the boy down and gently pinching his little face. "Those fish were so fat, covered in green scales, and incredibly slippery."

His gaze drifted, staring blankly into the distance as if he had fallen into some profound memory. 

"That fish was the most delicious fish your Third Uncle has ever eaten. It was a fish from heaven, an immortal among fishes..."

"How is the Wan family?" Li Mutian glanced down briefly, setting the clear tea in his hand onto the table, interrupting Li Xiangping.

Li Xiangping patted Li Xuanxuan, letting the child go play by himself, and raised his head to reply, "Their strength far exceeds ours, backed by a long and deep-rooted heritage. Their patriarch possesses Jade Capital Wheel cultivation..."

Just as he was speaking, Li Tongya stepped into the courtyard. His expression was grave as he found a seat and listened carefully to Li Xiangping's explanation.

Li Xiangping recounted everything Wan Yuankai had said in a single breath. 

"If this Wan family is truly beset from within and without, we should help them if we are able. The Ji family is right on our doorstep. If this Wan family really falls, without the lips, the teeth will grow cold; the next one will most likely be my Li family."

Only then did he pick up his tea bowl, taking several large gulps as he looked at the two of them.

Li Tongya silently listened to Li Xiangping's words, holding up the rough map to examine it.

Li Mutian narrowed his eyes and raised an eyebrow. He stared quietly at the tea in his cup before speaking. "The Wan family must be saved, but we must employ the right method—one that maximizes the benefit to our Li family. Xiangping, listen closely."

"The Wan family says the Ji family steals their spirit rice, so reply like this—tell them that we still have vacant spirit fields, but no manpower. Tell him to send some people to plant rice right here. My Li family will take thirty percent of the harvest. If Wan Yuankai truly sends people over, then his Wan family is indeed trapped by the Ji family and suffering unspeakably."

"Exactly."

Li Xiangping nodded, clearly sharing his father's line of thought, and said seriously, "It's just that this Ji family knows the Wan family's spirit fields like the back of their hand; their interior must have already become a sieve. This matter must be done in utmost secrecy by Wan Yuankai. Right now, we absolutely cannot clash with the Ji family; quietly pushing and supporting the Wan family from behind will be enough."

"Not bad," Li Mutian nodded in appreciation.

He took a light sip of tea and continued, "The strength of both families far exceeds ours. Right now, we still need to conceal our capacities and bide our time, accumulating our strength. The Ji family is aggressively pushing forward, and their intentions are malicious. As for the Wan family, although they are also under Azure Pond's rule, they are sleeping beside our bed, and we must likewise keep our guard up against them."

Li Tongya stared at the map for a long while before suddenly speaking, "Does the Ji family on this map share a border with us?"

"Yes." Li Xiangping nodded, tapping a spot at Lidaokou, and said in a deep voice, "Guli Road diverges here. One path goes north, directly entering the Ji family's territory, while the other goes east, leading into the Wan family's territory."

He paused, and seeing Li Tongya deep in thought, Li Xiangping continued, "I asked Wan Yuankai. Over a hundred years ago, a demon gathered a few lesser demons and built a demon cave on that path leading to the Ji family. As a result, that path was cut off. The Ji family abandoned both the path and that piece of land, only managing it in name."

"So that's how it is," Li Tongya realized. "In that case, we need not fear the Ji family crossing our borders. As long as the Wan family does not fall, the Ji family absolutely cannot threaten this place."

"That demon is quite smart, too. It chose the border between the Azure Pond Sect and the Metal Tang Gate, wholly focusing on cultivating inside its demon cave without coming out to cause trouble."

Li Xiangping spoke softly, turning his head to look at Li Mutian. "I heard from Wan Yuankai that the Mount Yue has been spotted to the west of our family. Father, when you fought with the Mount Yue back then, was there any information?"

Li Mutian waved his hand and said, "As for their appearance, those people of the Mount Yue have no difference from us, except they cut their hair and tattoo their bodies, and our languages do not communicate. Moreover, the Mount Yue love martial arts and are accustomed to battle, revering physical strength. Their tribe has those shamans who are skilled in curses and driving beasts, making them difficult to deal with."

"I don't know how effective your ⟨Evil Warding Art⟩ is. As long as we can handle those curses, the village militiamen and the enemy are about equal in strength, so it's not impossible to deal with them."

"There is no rush," Li Tongya spoke up.

His finger lightly drew a circle around the Li family on the map as he said, "The Wan family has suffered multiple squeezes and partitionings by the Ji family. The area Si Yuanbai demarcated for my Li family is also large. Today, our territory might even be larger than the Wan family's territory."

"Our land is vast, yet our people are scarce, and our strength is meager; we are merely swollen with fat, not truly robust. If the Mount Yue do not come, we have no need to provoke them. With the Wan family blocking in the front, let us develop for a few years and thoroughly master these territories before discussing anything else."

[^1]: A reference to the historic water towns located south of the Yangtze River in China. In Chinese literature and art, the Jiangnan region is synonymous with a highly romanticized, classical aesthetic characterized by whitewashed walls, dark gray roof tiles, misty rain, and serene waterways.
