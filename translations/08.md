# Chapter 8: Reception Method

The length of this ⟨Profound Pearl Spirit-Sacrificing Art⟩ far exceeded that of the ⟨Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture⟩. It primarily detailed how to use the mirror's body to draw upon the Supreme Yin lunar essence and condense a profound pearl talisman seed.

By planting this talisman seed in another person's dantian, it could assist them in their cultivation. When that person achieved success in their cultivation or perished, it would feed back to the benefactor's own body[^1], forging the Supreme Yin Profound Light.

Among its contents was also a ⟨Reception Method⟩, which contained instructions on how to request the profound pearl talisman seed from the mirror, and then nurture it within their dantian.

Lu Jiangxian was momentarily stunned. He felt that this ⟨Profound Pearl Spirit-Sacrificing Art⟩ looked rather wicked, much like the demonic arts described in books. Pondering over it carefully, he read the technique thoroughly from top to bottom and confirmed that this profound pearl talisman seed was not only harmless to the seeded individual but also possessed numerous benefits.

The ⟨Profound Pearl Spirit-Sacrificing Art⟩ was the mirror's innate awakened technique. The number of profound pearl talisman seeds he could condense depended on the strength of his divine sense. Currently, he could only bear the burden of six profound pearl talisman seeds.

Thinking up to this point, Lu Jiangxian shifted his divine sense, sweeping through the entirety of the Li family inside and out, and he could not help but sigh.

"What a massive change!"

He had slumbered within the mirror for three years. Today, the Li family had dug a pond and built a grand compound—how prosperous! Li Changhu had even taken a wife and grown a beard, looking every bit the new patriarch of the Li family.

He channeled the technique, sweeping it over the members of the Li family, and discovered—both expectedly and disappointingly—that not a single one possessed a spiritual aperture.

In other words, barring any miracles, the members of the Li family were fated to be devoid of immortal fortune for their entire lives, destined to be worn down by the mortal realm. If they were lucky, they might live to seventy and die a natural death.

Gazing at the Li family members around him, Lu Jiangxian let out a long sigh and channeled the technique.

"Father, look!" The Li brothers had already gathered around. Li Chejing was sharp-eyed and called out.

Li Mutian hurriedly focused his gaze. White light flickered on the mirror's surface, manifesting rows of tiny, neat characters.

He slapped Li Chejing's shoulder and urged repeatedly, "Bring silk cloth, a brush, and ink!"

The brothers quickly split up to fetch them. Fortunately, the Li family had built a study equipped with the four treasures, generously donated by the village teacher. Otherwise, they would have had to run all the way to the village entrance.

Before long, Li Changhu brought the cloth, and Li Tongya impressively carried over a wooden table outfitted with brush and ink. Thankfully, Li Chejing had been waiting by the mirror and had memorized the preceding text that surfaced and faded on the glass.

Among them, Li Chejing was the youngest in age but had read the most books. Li Mutian thus ordered him to wield the brush and transcribe all these tiny characters.

The ⟨Reception Method⟩ was quite long. Li Chejing wrote with the brush for the time it took an incense stick to burn until his wrist ached unbearably. They had to switch to Li Tongya to continue transcribing. The others watched from the side, terrified of copying a word wrong.

Taking turns like this, they transcribed continuously for two hours before finishing.

Fearing that they might practice the technique incorrectly, Lu Jiangxian displayed the text three full times, along with detailed annotations for some cryptic Daoist phrasing and several meridian circulation diagrams. After watching them meticulously verify the text, he finally slowly retracted the light.

"The ⟨Reception Method⟩."

Li Xiangping looked at the small characters on the silk scroll and read aloud slowly.

"The profound pearl is the true core. At the beginning of the first revolution, the core descends into the heart's meridians, hence it is said the core cavern lies in the central palace. The core's descent constitutes the first revolution... After the core descends, holding one's breath for nine intervals counts as one cycle, reaching nine times nine, eighty-one, equals nine cycles. Thus it is called the number of Double Nines..."

"This is an immortal art!"

Li Changhu was both shocked and overjoyed, wishing he could hug the mirror and plant a kiss on it.

Suppressing their ecstatic joy, the group passed the technique around, reading it carefully, and began silently memorizing it based on the annotations in the scroll.

Li Xiangping's eyes quickly swept over the silk cloth, landing on the final few lines at the end of the text.

"Having mastered the ⟨Reception Method⟩, await the days of Jiazi, Gengshen, one's birth year, the Three Epochs, the Three Gatherings, the Eight Festivals, or the days of the new and full moons. On these days, the heavenly qi proclaims life, and the vibrant yang dispels the yin. Place the dharma mirror beneath the lunar essence, burn incense, and bathe. Bow deeply and request, saying: 'I, disciple so-and-so of the such-and-such family, respectfully request the Profound Brilliance Wondrous Dharma, to govern my destiny and soothe my spirit, as I dedicate myself to pursuing the Dao. I shall perform meritorious deeds at the proper times, failing not in my efficacy and faith. As the talisman burns, my body offers its gratitude to the Supreme Yin.' Upon finishing, swallow the qi three times."

"I shall perform meritorious deeds at the proper times, failing not in my efficacy and faith. As the talisman burns, my body offers its gratitude to the Supreme Yin." He repeated in a low voice.

Li Mutian looked at his silent child with his head lowered, then patted his head comfortingly and spoke with a calm expression, "Govern my destiny and soothe my spirit, as I dedicate myself to pursuing the Dao."

---

In the early morning, wisps of cooking smoke drifted from the rooftops in the village, and a veil-like thin mist permeated the air.

"Uncle Li, is there some joyous occasion today?" Hearing Li Mutian humming cheerfully while walking through the fields, a farmer asked with a smile.

Li Mutian looked down and saw his nephew, Ye Chengfu. This young man looked honest and sincere, but he was also sweet-tongued.

"The green ears of grain in these fields are a joy to behold!" Li Mutian laughed heartily and replied.

There were four major surnames in Lijing Village: Ye, Tian, Li, and Liu. The Ye surname was the largest in Lijing Village, accounting for over thirty percent of the villagers. If they could twist together into a single rope, they would be a force to be reckoned with.

"Hah, that is brushing against Brother Changhu's joyful luck!" Ye Chengfu replied respectfully, leaning his hands on his hoe.

"You rascal, you sure know how to talk!"

The old man knew he was referring to Li Changhu's recent wedding. Lady Ren was lovely, obedient, and sensible, and her heart was completely devoted to Li Changhu.

Li Mutian was waiting for Lady Ren to give birth to an heir before appropriately revealing some information. She knew how to keep her mouth shut. Living under the same roof, being excessively secretive would instead breed resentment out of nowhere.

"It is Tongya who refuses to marry, and Xiangping has reached the right age as well." Li Mutian stroked his beard, counting the households in the village one by one.

_That little Tian Yun raised by my old brother is not bad, and her age is suitable too. I will go back and ask Xiangping. If it goes well, I will pick a good day and bring a wild goose to formally ask for her name,_ he thought with a smile.

Li Tongya and his brothers were carefully studying the ⟨Reception Method⟩ in the courtyard. Li Changhu had done a quick patrol of the fields in the morning and then hurried back to study alongside them.

It was only he, this old man, who felt that his years were advanced and his mind not as sharp as before, so he had set aside the immortal art to watch over the fields.

Firstly, mundane affairs always needed someone to manage them, otherwise, there would be no source for their food and expenses. Secondly, it was also to guard against any blind fools secretly climbing the walls to spy on them.

Li Mutian was in an excellent mood today. He sat unhurriedly on the ridge of the field, chatting idly with the old men of the village.

---

The Li family.

Lu Jiangxian's divine sense fell upon Li Tongya and the other three, who were sitting cross-legged in the courtyard with their eyes closed. Faintly discernible white bristles of light suddenly appeared atop their heads.

Li Chejing's white bristle was the longest, about eight inches, shining brightly. Li Tongya and Li Xiangping measured six and five inches respectively, while Li Changhu's was merely three inches long.

The white bristle manifested during the initial practice of the ⟨Reception Method⟩ represented a person's compatibility with the profound pearl talisman seed. If someone with a one-foot white bristle swallowed the talisman seed, they would cultivate as swiftly as someone possessing a spiritual aperture.

With an eight-inch white bristle, Li Chejing could achieve eighty percent of the cultivation speed of someone with a spiritual aperture. Li Changhu's bristle, however, was merely three inches, meaning his cultivation speed was only thirty percent of an aperture-holder.

In other words, Li Changhu cultivating for an entire day would not even match another person cultivating for four hours...

[^1]: The term used is *fǎnbǔ* (反哺), which literally means "reverse-feeding." It's a metaphor for filial piety—like a young crow feeding its parents—meaning the energy is "returning the favor" and flowing back to nourish the original source.
