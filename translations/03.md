# Chapter 3: The Mirror

"Ah, Brother Xiangping."

Li Xiangping was walking back with his basket on his back when a girl approached from afar. She had a round face and rather ordinary features, yet her brimming smile added a great deal of charm to her appearance.

"Sister Yun."

Li Xiangping looked at the girl with a smile, turning to hand over his basket of the day's catch.

"Look at the fine fish your brother caught. You should take a few back to taste."

"I couldn't possibly"

Tian Yun smiled and lowered her head. Girls matured early; at eleven years old, she had already grown taller than the thirteen-year-old Li Xiangping.

In Lijing Village, men and women usually married between the ages of thirteen and fifteen. Among their peers, they were the most suitable match in age, and in her heart, the girl had already determined the boy before her to be her future husband.

"Nonsense, take them!" Forcing two fish into Tian Yun's hands, Li Xiangping thought nothing more of it. Among the villagers, Uncle Tian was the most honest and generous, and Li Xiangping liked him best. Encountering his daughter, it was only natural to take care of her.

Bidding farewell to Tian Yun, Li Xiangping hurried into his house and placed the basket into the small pond.

After a moment's thought, he took out the mirror, wiped it clean, and tucked it into his robes. He then picked up three wooden boxes from the table and headed for the fields, where his two older brothers were working with his father.

The Li family had four brothers: Li Changhu, Li Tongya, Li Xiangping, and Li Chejing. In Lijing Village, whenever these four brothers were mentioned, there was no one who did not raise a thumb in praise. Tian Yun's father, whenever speaking of them, would always say with envy, "Li Mutian is truly blessed with good fortune!"

The blessed Li Mutian, however, did not share this sentiment. As the only person in Lijing Village who had traveled far from home, Li Mutian felt endless sorrow watching his children toil in the fields.

"A good man should study texts and command soldiers; to languish in the fields is to be a coward!" he once scolded, pointing at Uncle Tian.

Yet such is life; those who have glimpsed the light suffer all the more for it. Li Mutian had served in the military and had slain men. Returning to Lijing Village in his forties, he used his military pay to buy land and became a wealthy patriarch, but he remained the most dissatisfied of all.

When Li Xiangping reached the edge of the fields, his eldest brother Li Changhu was already waiting in the shade of a tree. Being the oldest at seventeen, a light beard already graced his upper lip.

"Walk slowly, Third Brother. There is no rush."

Li Changhu smiled and rubbed Li Xiangping's head, looking at him with gentle affection.

"I heard from Uncle Tian that you had a great harvest today."

"Too much, brother!" Li Xiangping laughed heartily. "Tonight we can finally eat a fine meal!"

"You."

Li Changhu wiped the sweat from him, picked up a wooden box, and called out toward the fields, "Second Brother!"

"Coming!" Second Brother Li Tongya walked over briskly with his hoe. He sat down first, called out to Li Changhu as Eldest Brother, and then turned a smiling gaze upon Li Xiangping.

"You two eat. I must head back." Having been busy all morning, Li Xiangping was ravenously hungry, and hurriedly made his way home.

---

Inside Li Xiangping's basket, Lu Jiangxian vaguely felt an inexplicable pulling force attracting him. As they drew closer to the Li household, this sensation grew increasingly intense.

Passing the large locust tree at the village entrance, the attraction reached its peak upon entering the settlement. Lu Jiangxian felt a stifling tightness, the mirror's body trembling faintly within the basket, emitting a red glow from its surface.

_That is a part of me, or perhaps something of vital importance to me._ A profound understanding dawned in Lu Jiangxian's heart.

_It is to the north! In the direction of that great lake!_ As Li Xiangping slowly distanced himself from the village entrance, the attraction gradually faded. Lu Jiangxian silently committed the direction to memory.

Having been carried by Li Xiangping for a lap around the village, and by combining his perception of mental activities with observations of the locals' movements and tones, Lu Jiangxian was largely able to comprehend what everyone he encountered was saying.

He discovered that this appeared to be nothing more than an ordinary small village. There were no martial dao experts leaping over roofs and scaling walls, nor did he see any cultivators soaring through the sky.

The people dutifully worked at sunrise and rested at sunset; neither the tools they used nor the scale of their fields showed anything beyond expectation.

_This place truly seems to be just an ordinary small village. The best dwelling is merely a two-story earth-and-wood structure..._ Lu Jiangxian pondered. _If there were cultivators, surely they would not live in a small earthen hut, would they?_

_Great power would bring about great productivity. The small village before me is far too backward._

He now had a general grasp of his situation, and a framework for his future plans slowly took shape.

The Li household was exceptionally busy tonight. The mother and the two younger lads were occupied with washing vegetables and cooking.

Li Xiangping had come home fully loaded, while the youngest brother, Li Chejing, had secretly caught a nest of plump field mice and stuffed them into a grain sack while picking mulberry leaves for silkworms in the back mountain. This so delighted their father, Li Mutian, that he gave the two young men a heavy, hearty pat on the shoulders, praising them until they beamed with boundless pride.

Fourteen and fifteen were the exact ages for physical growth; the several dishes on the table mostly went into the boys' stomachs.

Li Mutian only took a single taste with his chopsticks, and the mother, Liu Linyun, watched the four brothers with a radiant smile. Only the big yellow dog paced anxiously beneath the table, weaving between the legs of the six people.

After dinner was finished.

The moon slowly climbed over the Great Li Mountains. Li Xiangping leaned against the base of the wall with a long stalk of barnyard grass in his mouth, while his father, Li Mutian, furrowed his brows and gazed at the sky, as if searching for something.

"By the way, Father." Li Xiangping patted his father's trouser leg and pulled the mirror from his robes.

"I picked up this from the river early this morning."

"Oh?" Li Mutian took the mirror, narrowed his eyes, and turned it over and over in his hands.

"It is neither iron nor copper. What manner of treasure is this?"

The cold, clear moonlight slowly drifted down before the house. As if coming alive, it gathered bit by bit above the surface of the mirror, forming a faint lunar halo. Li Xiangping could not help but rub his eyes; it was as if the most beautiful moonlight he had seen in his thirteen years of life was manifesting upon that single halo.

"Father!" Li Xiangping whispered urgently.

"Shut your mouth!" Li Mutian paled in sheer terror, forcing himself to tear his gaze away from the lunar halo. His hands trembling uncontrollably, he shoved the mirror back into Li Xiangping's robes and whispered low into his ear, "Hide inside. Tell your brothers to grab their blades and come out."

For the first time, Li Xiangping saw such ruthless, murderous intent on his father's face. His eyes were narrowed slightly, resembling a hawk poised to strike.

"Yes..."

His voice trembled and his legs turned to jelly as he inched his way through the door.

The night was still fair as Li Xiangping pushed open the door to the room.

"Brothers... Father says to quickly grab your blades and head out."

Li Xiangping nearly cried out, speaking in a quivering whisper to his two older brothers resting against the headboard.

"What?!"

Li Changhu stood up in shock. He gripped Li Xiangping by the shoulders with both hands, looking at him with deep concern. "Is Father injured? Did he say what the matter is?"

Second Brother Li Tongya, however, rolled out of bed, retrieving two blades from underneath it and taking down the rattan armor and long staffs hanging on the wall.

He cast a gloomy look out the window, and muttered, "Enemies must have come to our door."

He handed over a long blade and a set of rattan armor, kept a long staff in his own hands, and patted Li Changhu.

"Brother, there is no need to ask further. Third Brother, take Mother and Fourth Brother and hide in the rear courtyard."

"Alright."

Li Xiangping hastily went to find his mother. Li Changhu also quickly calmed down, donned his rattan armor, and walked toward the door, blade in hand.

Outside the house.

Li Mutian stood at the doorway, quietly gazing at the melon patch in front of the house. Waiting until both youths had taken their positions behind him, he waved his hand and took one of the long blade.

"The two of you, search the front and back of the house, one to the left and one to the right, to ensure no one is around." The two hastily voiced their compliance and split up left and right to investigate.

Li Mutian himself boldly strode into the melon patch in front of the house. Bending over, he lifted his arms and, astonishingly, hauled a person out from the melon vines.
