# Chapter 18: Three Years

Lijing Village had been in an uproar late into the night, yet by cockcrow, all the villagers were awake. The early autumn morning air was sweet and fresh, the dew crystal clear, but a bleak desolation hung over the village.

The Liu and Li families distributed some dry rations. The refugees huddled at the village entrance to eat, while the middle-aged leader looked anxiously at the twenty or so people beside him, thinking to himself, _This is truly a misery that cannot be voiced. Having killed their leader, let alone staying, we cannot even manage to leave._

A commotion rippled through the refugees as they raised their heads to look toward the mountain path.

Someone shouted, "Someone is on the path! Look at that old man!"

They saw a grey-haired old farmer walking down the winding road. His clothes and arms were mottled with bloodstains. With his left hand, he struggled to drag a bloody corpse, while the hoe resting on his right shoulder bore a mangled, loose-haired human head.

"It's the assassin!"

Those with sharp eyes recognized the clothing on the headless corpse and could not help but feel a chill run down their spines. He had only escaped in the night, yet by morning, his head had been claimed. They did not know who this old man was, but the Li family's might was truly terrifying.

Seeing Old Man Xu approach, the people on both sides hurriedly backed away, clearing a path for him.

Old Man Xu's face was pale, his eyes devoid of light. He marched forward without casting a single glance at the crowd around him, walking entirely in his own world.

By the time he shuffled to the Li family courtyard at the end of the village, a tenant had already gone ahead to deliver the news. The Li family opened their gates, and Li Mutian, looking utterly exhausted, led his family out to receive him.

"Uncle Xu, what is this..."

"That remnant of the Yuan family... I have killed him. His corpse and head are both here. Call Liu Linfeng and Tian Shoushui to identify him."

Old Man Xu hunched his back, his hands and feet freezing cold. Dropping the corpse to the ground, he sat down and gasped for breath.

Li Tongya hastily brought tea from the inner room and offered it to Old Man Xu, but the old man's limbs trembled so violently he could not even hold the cup. He could only sip from it as Li Tongya held it to his lips.

Before long, Liu Linfeng and Tian Shoushui arrived alongside Old Man Xu's eldest son. Old Man Xu recounted the entire sequence of events before the crowd. After several rounds of confirmation, it was undeniably proven that this man was the Yuan family remnant.

"Uncle Xu, Eldest Brother's great vengeance has been avenged. The Li family is endlessly grateful..."

Li Tongya was saying with red eyes, but Old Man Xu tiredly waved his hand, speaking through his tears, "You need not thank me. I received Hu'er's kindness, so I killed this man in his stead. I will absolutely not use this favor to scheme for your Li family's wealth. This old man does not have many years left to live. If you truly wish to express your gratitude, then wait until that child is born, and bring him to see me."

Having spoken, he forced himself to stand. Ignoring the Li family's pleas for him to stay, he leaned on his eldest son and departed.

The Li family's mourning affairs lasted for several days, the courtyard draped in white cloth and mourning garbs. In life, Li Changhu had treated others generously and had been fiercely loyal, thus the sound of weeping could be heard from every household.

Burdened by these affairs, Li Tongya's preparations for breakthrough were delayed time and time again. It was not until two months after Li Changhu's burial that his state of mind finally stabilized, allowing him to condense the Profound Scenery Wheel and step through the gates of immortal cultivation.

---

Two years later.

The bright morning sunlight cast over a few sparse saplings in the courtyard, scattering fragmented shadows across the grounds. Beneath a tree sat a handsome young man, his legs crossed as he focused entirely on breathing and refining qi.

A short while later, he exhaled a long breath of turbid air, retracted his incantations, and looked into the courtyard with a smile.

A boy of about two years old, clutching a handful of wildflowers, came bounding into the rear courtyard.

Smiling happily, he babbled, "Uncle... hug..."

Li Chejing smiled, gently reaching out his hands to lift the child high into the air. Pressing his forehead against the child's little head, he asked warmly, "Has Xuan'er been a good, obedient boy today?"

"Want... hug..." The child ignored him, giggling as he squirmed in Li Chejing's hands.

"Xuan'er! Come out here!"

Lady Ren did not dare to enter the rear courtyard, calling out in a low voice from the entrance.

Setting the child on the ground, Li Chejing watched with a smile as the boy bounced into his mother's embrace.

He then said softly, "This Heavenly Circuit Wheel is truly difficult to cultivate. After a full year and a half, it is finally about to be completed!"

"Jing'er, you are truly insatiable!"

Standing up behind him, Li Xiangping chided with a soft laugh, "We have only just reached the second level of Fetal Breathing, the Bearing Brightness Wheel, and haven't even touched the fringes of the third level's Heavenly Circuit Wheel. Yet here you are complaining that your cultivation is too slow and wasting your time!"

Li Chejing chuckled. Without answering him, he continued on his own, "Tonight, I will begin condensing the Heavenly Circuit Wheel. I will let you all see what it truly means to have dharma power flow incessantly in a heavenly circuit."

"You child."

Li Xiangping laughed out loud. Seeing Li Mutian walk into the rear courtyard with his hands behind his back, he lowered his head and called out, "Father."

Li Mutian had indeed changed significantly over these past two years. His hair had turned grey, his wrinkles had deepened, and he kept a stern face all day, maintaining a rigid, unsmiling demeanor. He looked as though he had aged more than ten years.

"This child Xuanxuan is truly mischievous!"

Seeing his grandson, Li Xuanxuan, a hint of a smile finally graced Li Mutian's face. Following Li Changhu's death, the old man had lost all appetite and was constantly lethargic. The birth of this child had undoubtedly injected new life into him, allowing him to pull himself together.

As soon as Li Xuanxuan was born, Li Mutian had climbed out of bed and gathered the Li family. He tasked Li Xiangping with finding three incantations from the ⟨Reception Method⟩ to serve as the generational naming order for the Li family moving forward.

Li Xiangping deliberated carefully for several days and selected three verses describing Fetal Breathing from the manual.

These three verses were:

"The Profound Scenery rests in the clear abyss, where sun and moon Bear Brightness.  
The Heavenly Circuit traverses the Crimson Palace, giving voice to the Azure Essence.  
The Jade Capital reflects the celestial image, witnessing only the Nascent Spirit."

The children of Li Tongya, Li Xiangping, and the others would draw their names from the words 'Profound Scenery' (Xuanjing). Boys would take 'Xuan', and girls would take 'Jing', and so on.

Li Mutian also consulted Lady Ren. Ren Ping'er pondered for a night and chose the character 'Xuan,' meaning to declare or proclaim. Thus, the name of Li Changhu's posthumous child became: Li Xuanxuan.

"It is rather that Father dotes on him too much." Li Tongya smiled and shook his head, placing the wooden slip in his hand onto the rack as he replied.

"Nonsense!" Li Mutian feigned anger, blowing his beard and glaring as he shouted, before his expression turned serious, "I want Li Yesheng to follow by my side and learn more."

"Li Yesheng?"

Li Tongya lowered his head to ponder for a few breaths before saying, "That is indeed a good method. Li Yesheng is all alone in the world and is exceptionally close to our family. As we must cultivate our incantations and breathe in spiritual energy, we will have no time to manage mundane, worldly affairs. Since Li Yesheng is also of the Li family bloodline, he couldn't be more suitable."

"I only fear that over time, this man might develop selfish desires and line his own pockets..." Li Chejing asked with a frown.

"I can still live for another ten years; I can at least keep him in check!" Li Mutian's tone was unyielding, and he coldly continued, "Once the children and grandchildren grow up in ten years, it won't even be his turn to entertain such improper thoughts!"

"Governing people still requires a balance of grace and authority. Once Li Yesheng marries and has children, everything will be easy to manage," Li Xiangping stated plainly.

"Exactly." Li Chejing retrieved a wooden slip from the rack, blew off the dust, and chuckled softly, "Ten years of immortal cultivation—it truly makes one look forward to it."

**Translator's Note: Generational Names**

In traditional Chinese clan structures, a generational name is specific character shared by all siblings and cousins of the same generation. 

These characters are sequentially drawn from a predetermined "generation poem."

The current naming scheme for the Li family, established by Li Mutian draws its generation poem directly from the ⟨Reception Method⟩.

```markdown
XuanJing YuanQing, XiYue ChengMing.
ZhouXing JiangQue, SuiYu QingYuan.
YuJing YingXiang, WeiJian LingChu.
```
