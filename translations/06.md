# Chapter 6: Jade

Li Tongya and Li Xiangping entered the reed marshes. Pushing aside the overlapping layers of reeds to take a closer look, they saw the grayish-blue mirror emitting a warm, glowing white light.

Li Xiangping checked their bearings and whispered, "This is the direction of Moongaze Lake. It is but an hour's walk alongside Guli Road."

Li Tongya shook his head and said, "We cannot take Guli Road. We must cut through the reed marshes."

Li Xiangping murmured his agreement, and crouching low, he moved forward with his older brother.

Lu Jiangxian only felt the pulling force growing stronger. As they entered the reed marshes, his vision brightened. A hazy scene appeared before him—a seemingly cool, crystal-clear lake, where a dozen white egrets rested on one leg along the shore.

As Li Xiangping pressed forward, the grayish-blue mirror in his hands grew hotter, searing enough to make him restless. He looked up at his second brother. Li Tongya maintained a cold silence, yet his eyes were brimming with anxiety.

_Can mortals truly lay their hands upon immortal fortune..._ Li Xiangping stroked the mirror, entirely oblivious to the scalding heat.

"Good mirror, sweet mirror, we are almost there," he muttered, cradling the grayish-blue precious mirror.

Before long, the two emerged from the dense, overlapping reeds. The sparkling waters of Moongaze Lake and a flock of startled egrets taking flight immediately caught their eyes.

Lu Jiangxian stared straight at a rocky shoal in the middle of the lake. Amidst those moss-covered, scattered rocks, a piece of white-glowing jade was tightly wedged in a crevice.

The scorching grayish-blue mirror spewed forth a pale streak of lunar essence. A blurry image of the shoal and the white-glowing jade wedged in the rocks floated before the eyes of Li Tongya and Li Xiangping.

Li Xiangping and Li Tongya exchanged a glance, both seeing the astonishment and joy in each other's eyes. Li Xiangping nodded vigorously, stripped off his clothes, and was about to step into the lake.

"Hold on!" Li Tongya pulled him back and shook his head.

"I will fetch it. You hold the mirror and wait on the shore. If the moon reaches that position and I have not returned,"

He pointed at a position in the night sky.

"Then hide the mirror in the reeds and run toward Guli Road. Do not go home," Li Tongya said, staring solemnly at his younger brother. "Wait until the sun is high in the sky tomorrow before coming back to check."

"Yes..." Li Xiangping unknowingly choked up, wiping away his tears as he looked at his second brother.

Li Tongya gave a small, reassuring smile as he removed his clothes, revealing a lean, muscular upper body. He turned and swam into the lake, leaving Li Xiangping hiding in the reeds, staring blankly after him.

He had visited Moongaze Lake with his father and brothers in previous years, swimming back and forth more times than he could count. Knowing the way well, he easily treaded water to the center of the lake.

Carefully probing through the numerous rocky crevices, he thoroughly searched the entire shoal within the span of an incense stick's burning.

_Nothing._ Li Tongya frowned. He meticulously checked once more, pulling out five or six small river crabs in the process.

Sure enough, a faint coolness touched his fingertips as they brushed against something smooth. Pinching his fingers together, he pulled out a piece of jade about two fingers wide.

The jade was long and narrow, its surface carved with faint characters.

Li Tongya held it up to the moonlight to distinguish them, "Supreme... Moon... Qi... Nurturing Wheel..."

He could only roughly identify a few words. Li Tongya had learned to read from a teacher in his youth and recognized most common words, but the intricate patterns and archaic script on this jade made it exceedingly difficult to decipher.

Calmly looking toward the shore, he gripped the jade tightly and swam back.

"Third brother!"

He climbed ashore and called out softly into the reeds. Li Xiangping emerged. Just as Li Tongya was about to show him the jade, the moment he opened his hand, the jade transformed into a streak of white light and shot into the mirror with a swift hiss.

The sudden turn of events startled the brothers, causing them to shudder. They watched as white light surged across the mirror's surface, while the ambient moonlight coalesced into halos that plunged endlessly into the glass.

Lu Jiangxian, however, felt as if he had been struck by lightning. A blinding white light smashed directly into his face, and a vast, boundless ocean of knowledge flooded his mind. Crying out in pain, he promptly fainted.

Li Tongya and Li Xiangping watched as the mirror in their hands slowly calmed down after swallowing the jade, its white light gradually fading.

A golden-red dawn crawled into the sky, falling upon Li Tongya's bare upper body and highlighting his golden contours. He looked down at his brother and said, "Let us go home first."

---

Li Mutian sat by the red-lacquered wooden table. Having listened to his two sons explain the entire sequence of events, he nodded and said to Li Tongya, "You did well."

He and Li Changhu had tossed and turned all night, unable to sleep. Now, seeing the two boys return safely, the heavy stone in his heart finally settled.

"The front and back yards of our house are not small, and the back yard even rests against the rear mountain. I have been thinking of clearing the two melon patches out front to build two more houses, flanking the sides to form one large compound. Once the main gate is shut, we will not have to worry about anyone spying on us."

Li Mutian spoke unhurriedly. He had harbored this thought for years; his boys were growing into men day by day, and the time to divide the household was fast approaching.

Relying on his savings, the Li family could be considered a prominent and respectable household in the village. Upon returning from the army, Li Mutian had purchased over ten mu[^1] of paddy fields. Adding the five mu of fertile land inherited from his father, they had nearly twenty mu in total.

Lijing Village had excellent soil and water that nourished its people; the reed marshes and mudflats were lifesaving treasures.

If one subtracted the manual labor required, twenty mu of land in a bountiful year was more than enough to feed over a dozen mouths. The Li family had long reached the level where they could close their doors and live as proper landlords.

Precisely because the Li family possessed both grain and land, his four sons had the opportunity to learn how to read and write. Li Mutian had never been able to abide idle, rich families. Thus, he demanded that his sons split their days between studying and farming, ensuring that when the family was eventually divided, each would be able to eat well and live comfortably.

"But now, this family cannot be divided, and we must find wives who are intimately loyal to our own people."

Li Mutian recalled the grand households he had seen in the cities—a massive compound of direct kin, studying to become officials, practicing martial arts to lead armies. What a glorious sight! Poor farming households, on the other hand, divided themselves cleanly—you do not bother me, and I do not drag you down.

"Then it is settled!"

Li Mutian's eyes brightened as he addressed his two sons.

"Go to the fields and call your eldest brother back to level the foundation. Leave the fields for the tenant farmers to handle. Your fourth brother does not need to pick mulberry leaves this afternoon either; from now on, he will spend his entire days studying at the teacher's place."

"Understood." Despite tossing about all night, Li Xiangping was still full of vigor. At those words, he vanished out the door in a flash.

Li Tongya looked at his father, pondered for a moment, and then spoke, "Father, do you intend to emulate the clan structures written of in the books? Establishing an ancestral hall, opening a clan estate, studying for officialdom, and practicing martial arts for military command?"

"Our Li family has accumulated its foundation for two hundred years; it is about time."

Li Mutian smiled and waved his hand.

"As for studying and practicing martial arts, Guli Road is fraught with peril. Venturing in and out of the Great Li Mountains is a journey where nine out of ten perish. Even if one studies or practices martial arts, they cannot sell their skills to the Emperor of Yue. It is nothing more than seeking to pass down the family estate and ensuring our own self-preservation..."

Li Tongya nodded and said softly, "Perhaps there is something even more wondrous than studying and practicing martial arts."

"Cease your nonsense here."

Li Mutian laughed heartily, patted Li Tongya on the shoulder, and walked out with his chin raised and hands clasped behind his back.

[^1]: 1 Mu ≈ 0.1647 acres
    10 Mu ≈ 1.65 acres
