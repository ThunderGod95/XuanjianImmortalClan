# Chapter 14: Changhu and Xiangping

"Because he is sufficiently cruel."

Before Li Tongya's words had even settled, Li Changhu felt a chill crawl up his spine, his pupils dilating as the hairs all over his body stood on end. He stared at his younger brother's smiling face, a sense of dread settling in his heart.

"Hahahahahahaha..."

Giving Li Changhu’s shoulder a push, Li Tongya gently tossed the wooden slip in his hand and looked at Li Changhu with a light chuckle.

"You rascal." Li Changhu let out a long breath, assuming Li Tongya was merely joking. He waved his hand lightly and said, "I am off to prepare for Xiangping's wedding."

Having said this, Li Changhu straightened his clothes and walked out the door toward the Tian family.

Li Tongya, however, restrained his smile. Sitting quietly by the wooden table, he spoke, "Father."

Li Mutian had already appeared on the stone steps like a ghost. He stood there with a stony face, his thoughts unreadable. Hearing his name, he turned his head to look at Li Tongya.

Last night, while sitting before his door looking at the moon, Li Mutian had seen Li Xiangping dash toward the rear mountain in a fiery rush. Unable to set his heart at ease, he had quietly followed his son up the mountain.

Little did he know, Li Tongya had also been unable to set his mind at ease and had stealthily trailed his brother. When the two ran into each other, an awkward silence ensued. In tacit agreement, they watched Li Xiangping commit murder, and then personally witnessed the wild beasts devour the corpse cleanly before finally descending the mountain.

"Xiangping acted for the safety of our family. Father, you need not be angry..." Li Tongya consoled.

"As if I would be angry over that!"

Li Mutian's heart was heavy, and he spoke without restraint. Narrowing his eyes, he added, "The boy did well killing that piece of trash! If he had merely frightened him, I would have jumped down and hacked that trash to pieces myself! He did well killing him, extremely well! What is there for me to be angry about? Do not cover up for Li Changhu!"

Li Tongya let out a long sigh and spoke gloomily, "Eldest Brother is lenient and kind-hearted. The villagers and tenant farmers all respect him deeply. He is capable of protecting the family estate."

"Bullshit!" Li Mutian slammed his hand on the table, anger flashing across his face.

"The one they respect is me, Li Mutian! They surround your brother only because Li Changhu's rent is low! These people have always feared power while possessing no gratitude for benevolence. Look at the state of things—if I, Li Mutian, die today, Li Yecheng would have dared to come rolling up to our door tomorrow! Would Li Changhu dare kill him?"

Seeing Li Tongya lower his head in silence, Li Mutian's tone softened considerably as he whispered, "In the past, I was not afraid. With the two of you brothers supporting him, Li Changhu's leniency would have been perfect. By applying both grace and might, our future would have been full of promise. But things are different now. Our Li family harbors a treasure that invites doom; we are walking on thin ice. If the one leading the family lacks the ruthlessness to strike down threats, we will easily be annihilated!"

"Moreover," Li Mutian took a breath, his eyes filled with anxious worry.

"These past few days, a heavy unease has weighed upon my heart. I fear a calamity of death approaches."

---

After finalizing the numerous details of the marriage contract at the Tian household, Li Changhu sat blankly on the field ridge, his expression tangled. He had walked through the entire village today, yet Li Yecheng was the only person he had not crossed paths with.

Recalling his brother's low shout last night and Li Tongya's insinuation, Li Changhu realized that Yecheng had likely already been killed by Xiangping.

A wave of pain washed over Li Changhu's heart. He recalled how, when they were little, Yecheng and Tongya were just two tiny boys who would always obediently tail behind him down to the river to catch fish.

He vaguely remembered Yecheng holding a large black carp, calling out with a grin, "Eldest Brother, Eldest Brother, look at me!"

Tongya had clearly been envious but always turned his head, pouting, refusing to look at him. When the three of them grew tired from playing, they would stand by the river and pee, seeing who could pee the highest and the farthest.

But then their aunt died, and their second uncle fell ill. In just a few short months, Yecheng's temperament drastically changed, and he was never the same again.

"His crimes did not warrant death!"

A glint of moisture appeared in the corners of Li Changhu's eyes. He always felt that he could have persuaded him a little more, guided him to learn how to be a good person, to earnestly protect his younger brother Yesheng, and to live a proper life.

"Changhu!"

A call startled Li Changhu awake. He quickly lowered his head, wiped the corners of his eyes, and looked toward the source of the voice.

It was an old, white-haired farmer with an honest and simple appearance. His skin was tanned dark, and he wore homespun clothes with excessively wide pant legs.

"Uncle Xu," Li Changhu stood up, brushed off his pant legs, and asked with concern, "How are things? Do you have enough grain at home to eat?"

"Enough, enough!"

Seeing Li Changhu, tears began to fall from Old Man Xu's eyes. Although he was old, his body was still relatively robust. However, his only son had fallen severely ill and could not even tend to the fields properly.

Li Changhu had reduced his rent and gifted him grain, allowing them to barely scrape by. Old Man Xu was an elder who had watched Li Changhu grow up and had always viewed him as his own child. After this ordeal, his heart overflowed with immense gratitude.

"You absolutely must not be so polite!" Li Changhu quickly moved past his melancholy and spoke seriously, "My father instructed me a few days ago to take extra care of your fields, Old Xu, which is why I sought you out today."

Old Man Xu was wise to the ways of the world. How could he not know that Li Changhu was extending a favor on Li Mutian's behalf? With a gentle smile, he cupped his hands toward Li Changhu and laughed, "Please extend my deepest thanks to the Patriarch on my behalf! The Xu family has received this kindness."

"Ah, how can you speak of receiving kindness? It is what should be done!" Li Changhu waved his hand.

While the two chatted in the fields, Li Xiangping arrived at the edge of the reed marshes carrying a cloth bundle, silently watching Li Yesheng sitting on the bank.

Li Yesheng was counting on his fingers, muttering to himself, "I went to Third Uncle's place the day before yesterday, Eldest Uncle's place three days ago, and Fourth Uncle kicked me out yesterday. Today, all I can do is boil some wild vegetables, catch some shrimp, and make some soup to drink."

He looked out at the reed marshes swaying with the wind before him—the place that had birthed and nurtured him. Were it not for the generosity of these reed marshes, Li Yesheng would have long since died in some forgotten corner.

_And Eldest Uncle's family,_ he thought silently, only to see a figure emerge from the reeds.

"Brother Xiangping!"

Li Yesheng stood up in pleasant surprise. Ever since the main branch had built their grand courtyard, Li Xiangping rarely came out to walk around. It was said he spent his days reading in the courtyard; Li Yesheng only ever ran into him when he went to the main house to eat.

"Come, come, come."

Li Xiangping smiled, pulling a white steamed bun from his cloth sack and handing it to him. Li Yesheng quickly accepted it, wolfing it down in ravenous bites as he mumbled indistinctly, "You are the best to me, Brother!"

"Hahahahaha."

Li Xiangping and Li Yesheng were the closest. When they were little, Li Xiangping would secretly take food from home for Li Yesheng to eat. With Li Mutian turning a blind eye, Li Xiangping had indeed brought him quite a lot of things.

"Here." With a somewhat complex expression, Li Xiangping shoved the cloth bundle into Li Yesheng's hands and said, "These are the reading materials from when I first started learning. You can read them when you are out herding ducks."

"Thank you, Brother!"

Deeply moved, Li Yesheng scooped up a handful of river water to wash his hands before carefully stowing the cloth bundle away.

"In a few days, I will go ask Father to speak with the teacher. On days when you have nothing to do, you can go to his place and read more books. If you have any questions, you can always ask us."

"How could I impose!"

Overjoyed yet anxious, Li Yesheng repeatedly declined. His heart was filled with both unease and anticipation as he waved his hands frantically.

"I heard that studying requires paying tuition. My older brother will not give me any money."

Li Xiangping scrutinized him for a moment before saying flatly, "There is no need for such formalities between us. Asking my father simply means he will pay it for you."

"This..."

Li Yesheng wanted to say more, but looking at Li Xiangping's indifferent expression, a sense of reverence swelled within him for no apparent reason.

He thought to himself, _Brother Xiangping looks so much like Eldest Uncle!_

Unaware of Li Yesheng's thoughts, Li Xiangping patted his shoulder, instructed him on the matters of studying, and headed home.
