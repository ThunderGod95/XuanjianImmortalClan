# Chapter 10: Arranging a Marriage

Li Mutian, hands clasped behind his back, had just arrived at the front of the Tian family courtyard. Inside, Tian Yun, who was squatting and humming a tune while sorting vegetables, looked up.

Seeing it was Li Mutian, she smiled bashfully and said, "Uncle Li!"

Setting aside the vegetables in her hands, she hurriedly stood up and called out toward the house, "Father! Uncle Mutian is here."

"It is I." Li Mutian responded with a smile, looking Tian Yun up and down.

Over the past three years, this girl had blossomed beautifully. Her figure was full and gracefully curved. While she could not be considered stunning, her features were neat and proper, and her smile carried a unique, captivating charm.

"Not bad, not bad." Li Mutian brought forward the hand he had kept behind his back, revealing that he was carrying a large wild goose.

"Oh Uncle Li, you shouldn't have," Seeing Li Mutian bring a gift, Tian Yun was momentarily taken aback. 

Upon closer inspection, she exclaimed in surprise, "Is that a wild goose?"

The ⟦Book of Etiquette and Ceremonial on the Marriage Rites of the Ordinary Officer⟧ states: For the marriage rites, when the proposal is sent down and accepted, employ a wild goose.

In the Yue State, when a man's family wished to arrange a marriage with a woman's family, they would bring a wild goose to the door. This ritual was known as the formal proposal.

Small farming households could not adhere to overly complex rules. For an ordinary family in Lijing Village, let alone a wild goose, there were plenty who came empty-handed to propose. This was also Tian Yun's first time seeing a formal proposal, taking her a moment to realize what was happening.

"That Xiangping of my family, do you fancy him?"

Li Mutian laughed heartily, feeling the urge to tease her.

Tian Yun had already flushed red from the tips of her ears down to her neck. Out of modesty and shyness, she stammered, wanting to gloss over it. Yet, afraid that Li Mutian might take her evasion seriously, she had no choice but to say, "I do!"

Inside the house, Tian Shoushui had just stepped out the main door. Hearing this, he let out a massive sigh of relief; the stone that had long weighed upon his heart finally fell to the ground.

As a father, how could he not see that his daughter's heart had long been hitched to that Li Xiangping!

It was just that the boy was usually airtight in his demeanor, making it impossible for anyone to read his thoughts. Tian Shoushui feared his daughter would end up embarrassed, so he had remained completely silent on the matter.

"Eldest Brother!" Tian Shoushui called out with a loud laugh.

Tian Shoushui had followed behind Li Mutian since he was five years old. At the age of twelve, he had even enlisted in the army alongside Ren Ping'an. The three of them had banded together to survive in the military; they were not blood brothers, but their bond surpassed even that.

Later, after returning to the village, they slaughtered the wealthy Yuan family, washed their hands of the bloody past, and took up farming. Li Mutian had looked after him in every respect—distributing land to him and even acting as a matchmaker so he could take a wife.

He, Tian Shoushui, shared an inseparable bond of fortune and misfortune with the Li family. He had even personally helped raise Li Changhu, viewing him more dearly than his own child.

With his own daughter marrying into their family, how could she possibly suffer any grievances?

Tian Yun, entirely unaware of the numerous twists and turns in her father's thoughts, retreated hastily the momemt she saw him emerge, drawing another burst of laughter from Li Mutian.

"Eldest Brother, aside from this marriage, there is also an important matter," Tian Shoushui said, shaking his head helplessly, his expression turning serious.

"What is it?"

Li Mutian looked up, somewhat puzzled.

"A few days ago, when I was heading up the mountain, I passed by the Yuan family's ancestral tomb and heard some commotion. Yet, when I turned back to look, I saw absolutely nothing."

"Are there still surviving clansmen of the Yuan family that we failed to kill?" Li Mutian raised an eyebrow, his tone turning gloomy.

"I suspect it is merely some distant relatives or connections who dared to sneak in and offer sacrifices." Tian Shoushui felt an uneasy flutter in his heart and quickly tried to console himself.

"Indeed, what you say makes sense." Li Mutian gradually relaxed and nodded at Tian Shoushui. "Back then, we thoroughly investigated it. There were five members of the Yuan family; there was no mistake or omission."

"Eldest Brother, on such a joyous day, I should not have brought up such unlucky words!" Tian Shoushui gave himself a light slap on the mouth, amusing Li Mutian enough to elicit a soft chuckle.

Stepping out of the Tian family's home, Li Mutian walked along the dirt path with a calm expression, gazing thoughtfully at the Great Li Mountains.

---

Having concluded the marriage discussions, Li Mutian hummed a cheerful tune as he returned to the courtyard. Crossing the front yard, he found only three brothers chatting idly at the wooden table in the central patio.

His youngest son, Li Chejing, was in his room cultivating with his eyes closed. Although lunar essence was thin during the day, making progress slow, Li Chejing cultivated diligently day and night, refusing to waste even a sliver of time.

In the courtyard, Li Xiangping was repeatedly poring over the ⟨Reception Method⟩. The silk cloth had been thoroughly manhandled, covered in creases, with the ink smudging considerably.

"Third Brother, be a bit gentler with your tormenting," Li Tongya chided lightly with a smile, though the carving knife in his hand never ceased as he silently inscribed characters onto wooden slips.

The eldest brother, Li Changhu, was off to the side carefully calculating accounts against the land deeds.

Hearing this, he raised an eyebrow and said, "He has been tormenting it all morning."

Li Mutian stepped over the stone threshold, picked up a cup of clear tea from the wooden table, and sat right down on the steps, saying nonchalantly, "I went to the Tian family to propose marriage."

At those words, Li Xiangping leaped straight up from his chair, staring at his father with a face full of anxious urgency.

"What did the Tian family say?"

"Tian Yun said she fancies you." Li Mutian took a slow sip of his tea and let out a comfortable sigh.

"Good, good, that is wonderful." Li Xiangping nodded in excitement.

"Hahahahaha..." His two older brothers could not help but laugh.

"Listen here, all of you." Li Mutian, however, was thinking of other matters. He set down his teacup and adopted a stern tone.

"Though the path of immortality is said to be unfathomably wondrous, who knows what terrors and perils lie in wait? The Li family's numbers are already thin. If something happens to you, who will carry on the Li family legacy?"

Seeing the young men turn serious, Li Mutian waved his hand and continued, "This dharma mirror is our family's fortuitous encounter, but it could also be our family's ruin."

"I hastily arranged a marriage for Changhu and held the wedding with the hope that he would quickly produce an heir. It is precisely because I fear that, should a disaster occur, our Li family might still possess a sliver of hope for survival."

"The same goes for you." Li Mutian pointed at Li Xiangping, then looked at Li Tongya and sighed helplessly.

"As for Tongya... I am old, and I can no longer sway you. You have no interest in the women of this village, but you must still leave behind an heir..."

Li Tongya nodded in silence. It took a few breaths before he finally spoke, "Rest assured, Father. I know the measure of things."

"That is for the best."

Li Mutian brushed a hand over the white hair at his temples, his heart swirling with anxiety.

He was nearly sixty years old. Though his body remained robust and he had no worries over food and clothing, the affairs of the world were impermanent. It was better to arrange the family's matters as early as possible!

"Father!"

A crisp, clear shout interrupted the somewhat oppressive atmosphere in the courtyard. Li Chejing dashed out of the house and stood firmly before his older brothers.

"I am about to finish refining eighty-one wisps of lunar essence and condense the Profound Scenery Wheel!" Li Chejing raised his head with pride.

"My Chejing is truly formidable."

Li Mutian had long heard his youngest son babble endlessly about the gateway to the Fetal Breathing realm and the Profound Scenery Wheel. Overjoyed, he pulled him into a hug and laughed heartily as he looked at the child.

The brothers were also infected by the joyous atmosphere, smiles breaking out on their faces. Li Changhu even pinched Li Chejing's cheeks, not letting go until the boy cried out in repeated complaint.

"The next time we can receive the talisman seeds will not be until the Summer Solstice."

Li Tongya smiled as he watched the joyful scene in the courtyard, pondering to himself, _We missed the days of the dark and new moons because we were ill-prepared. The next occasion is the Summer Solstice; we merely have to wait a few more days._

He quietly continued to carve the ⟨Reception Method⟩ onto the wooden slips, yet his heart surged with tempestuous waves of unbridled excitement.

_The path of immortal cultivation—Li Tongya has arrived._
