# Chapter 9: White Pill

The moon hung above the willow branches.

Li Chejing suddenly opened his eyes, turning to look at Li Mutian.

The old man sat to the side drinking tea while holding the silk cloth recording the ⟨Reception Method⟩.

"Father, over these two months, I have committed the incantation to memory," he paused before adding. "Today is the Start of Summer, one of the Eight Festivals. Perhaps I may give it a try."

"Go ahead, then." Li Mutian nodded, speaking slowly.

Li Chejing was overjoyed. His three older brothers were also full of anticipation, helping him burn incense and bathe before setting up an altar table in the courtyard.

Li Mutian then respectfully brought out the dharma mirror, propping it up on a mirror stand carved with hornless dragon motifs that he had personally crafted a few days prior. In front of the altar, he planted nine sticks of incense and arranged offerings of fruit.

Lu Jiangxian could not help but feel a drop of sweat. With the incense and fruit offerings on the altar, it truly looked as if they were worshipping him like a dead man.[^1]

Before the altar, Li Chejing performed three kneelings and nine kowtows.

Kneeling with his head bowed low, he spoke with utmost reverence, "I, disciple Li Chejing of the Li family, respectfully request the Profound Brilliance Wondrous Dharma, to govern my destiny and soothe my spirit, as I dedicate myself to pursuing the Dao."

"I shall perform meritorious deeds at the proper times, failing not in my efficacy and faith. As the talisman burns, my body offers its gratitude to the Supreme Yin."

Having spoken, he emptied his mind, channeled the technique, and repeatedly consumed the natural qi of supreme harmony.

Simultaneously, Lu Jiangxian shifted his thoughts. The surface of the mirror immediately overflowed with radiant, colorful light, rippling like water.

"There is a reaction!"

Li Mutian and the others were greatly heartened, their eyes fixed intently upon the altar.

The grayish-blue mirror buzzed, and a white pill leaped from its surface. It was perfectly round and brilliantly luminous, its flashing white light illuminating the courtyard in a vast expanse of white, so bright that the onlookers could not open their eyes.

Li Chejing only felt his mind go entirely blank amid a deafening roar.

Suddenly, a majestic, resonant voice surfaced within, "Here is a disciple of the Li family, who has restrained his passions, blocked his transgressions, and severed the roots of evil. I bestow upon him the Profound Brilliance Wondrous Dharma, that his Daoist karma may blossom, allowing him to pass from the mortal to the sacred. From beginning to end, starting with the precepts and talismans, he shall begin his ascent to truth. I hereby grant one scroll of the Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture, and one path to the Golden Light Art."

Li Chejing hurriedly scrambled up from the ground and sat cross-legged. The white pill bounded upward and vanished into his Niwan Palace[^2]. His entire body immediately jolted, submerged in the complex information flooding his mind.

Having imparted the incantations, the white pill gently spun within the Niwan Palace before travelling along his meridians, finally settling within the Qihai aperture[^3] of his lower dantian.

Watching the Profound Pearl Talisman Seed sink into the top of Li Chejing's head, Li Mutian and the others instantly became incredibly tense. With their hearts in their throats, they watched Li Chejing sit cross-legged, eyes closed to preserve his spirit, remaining in that posture until dawn.

When the Supreme Yin fell and the Golden Crow rose, Li Chejing finally shuddered and slowly awoke. Opening his eyes, he saw a circle of his brothers surrounding him, their expressions filled with anxious unease.

"Father! Brothers! I have received the profound pearl talisman seed and have already crossed the threshold of immortal cultivation!" Li Chejing leaped up in excitement, hugging his father with overwhelming emotion.

Li Mutian chuckled happily, spinning Li Chejing around in a circle. Li Tongya and the others let out a long breath of relief, their expressions also turning to excitement and thrill.

"I received an incantation called the Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture."

Having said that, Li Chejing was about to recite the outline of the incantation. However, the white pill in his Qihai aperture gave a gentle throb, and in an instant, he lost his voice, merely babbling without being able to articulate a single word.

Pale with shock, he instinctively covered his mouth and called out again, "Father!"

Seeing that he could speak once more, Li Chejing let out a long sigh of relief. Refusing to believe it, he tried again to recite the scripture's outline, only to find that he could neither speak it aloud nor write it from memory.

"This immortal art can neither be written down nor spoken aloud. How truly miraculous." Li Chejing's expression shifted slightly, revealing a look of distress.

"There is no need to be tense." Li Changhu smiled and rubbed Li Chejing's head.

Watching their father reverently return the dharma mirror from the table, he added, "When the Summer Solstice arrives, the rest of us will request the dharma together."

"Eldest Brother," Li Chejing interrupted him, speaking with hesitation. "It seems there are only six of these talisman seeds."

"Only six?" Li Tongya, who had been holding the ⟨Reception Method⟩ and reading it carefully, looked at him upon hearing this, his voice laced with doubt.

"When I received the profound pearl talisman seed, many things hazily appeared in my mind—something about the six stages of immortal cultivation, something about the Fetal Breathing Wheel-Nurturing method. It seems this dharma mirror can only separate out six talisman seeds," Li Chejing explained frankly.

Li Xiangping nodded, offering comfort. "Such a miraculous artifact harnesses the creative fortunes of heaven and earth. Naturally, its numbers would not be many."

The eldest brother, Li Changhu, yawned and waved his hand, telling his brothers, "Let us all go back to sleep. We have waited all night and are weary."

"You are just afraid Sister-in-law will worry!" Li Xiangping laughed heartily, teasing him.

"You rascal!" Li Changhu replied unabashedly, pointing at Li Xiangping with a smile before dropping his voice.

"I think Tian Yun is quite nice."

"Eldest Brother, do not play the foolish matchmaker!" Li Xiangping stiffened his face, turned his head, and stormed out of the courtyard, heading back to his room.

"Hahahahaha..." Li Changhu walked into the front courtyard with a loud laugh.

_There is hope!_ Li Mutian stroked his beard, musing internally.

---

That night, the moonlight flowed like water.

Li Chejing sat cross-legged in the courtyard with his eyes closed. Forming a hand seal, he drew a current of qi from his Qihai aperture, letting it wander through his meridians, vaulting over the Twelve-Story Pagoda[^4] at his throat and neck, before surfacing between his brows.

In an instant, a brightness flared before Li Chejing's eyes. The water-like moonlight slowly drifted toward the space between his brows, merging with that wisp of spiritual energy.

Before long, Li Chejing formed a seal, guiding this current of qi—which had now transformed into a milky white hue—to fall into his brow, cross the Twelve-Story Pagoda, and drop back into the Qihai aperture.

After doing this three times, Li Chejing finally opened his eyes. He looked up at the sky; four hours had already passed.

"This speed of absorbing lunar essence is far too slow."

Li Chejing calculated carefully. Absorbing lunar essence eighty-one times would refine a single wisp of lunar essence qi. A full eighty-one wisps were required to form the first of the Six Wheels of Fetal Breathing—the Profound Scenery Wheel.

Once the Profound Scenery Wheel was formed, he would be considered to have entered the Fetal Breathing realm and could cast arts.

_Three times takes four hours, so eighty-one times will be one hundred and eight hours,_ Li Chejing figured in his mind. _If I cultivate for twelve hours a day, it will take nine days. Eighty-one wisps will take a full two years..._

Gazing at the lunar essence gathering upon the dharma mirror, Li Chejing blinked. A spark of inspiration hit him, and he sat cross-legged by the stone altar to cultivate.

Within the mirror, Lu Jiangxian was half-asleep, whiling away the time. He had long since absorbed a sufficient amount of lunar essence. Time inside the mirror was long and tedious. Much like listening to a boring filler class in college, Lu Jiangxian would doze off in a daze; sometimes, he would close his eyes and an entire day would pass.

Li Chejing's spiritual energy leaped from his brow and fell into the lunar halos upon the mirror, instantly jolting Lu Jiangxian awake. Looking at the little boy before him, he could not help but laugh inwardly.

"How remarkably clever."

Li Chejing guided the spiritual energy back into his Qihai aperture. He felt a cool sensation wash over his entire body; that lunar essence's power had expanded by over tenfold. Looking at it this way, he only needed to do this five or six times to form a wisp of lunar essence qi!

He could not help but feel immensely uplifted. Forming the hand seals once more, he continued his cultivation. It was only when the rosy hues of dawn filled the sky and his brothers arrived at the rear courtyard that he reluctantly concluded his session.

Seeing his brothers and father all gathered, Li Chejing recounted the events of the previous night. Everyone was both shocked and delighted. Sighing, Li Mutian offered another stick of incense to Lu Jiangxian before leading the children out of the hidden room.

# Chapter 10: Arranging a Marriage

Li Mutian, hands clasped behind his back, had just arrived at the front of the Tian family courtyard. Inside, Tian Yun, who was squatting and humming a tune while sorting vegetables, looked up.

Seeing it was Li Mutian, she smiled bashfully and said, "Uncle Li!"

Setting aside the vegetables in her hands, she hurriedly stood up and called out toward the house, "Father! Uncle Mutian is here."

"It is I." Li Mutian responded with a smile, looking Tian Yun up and down.

Over the past three years, this girl had blossomed beautifully. Her figure was full and gracefully curved. While she could not be considered stunning, her features were neat and proper, and her smile carried a unique, captivating charm.

"Not bad, not bad." Li Mutian brought forward the hand he had kept behind his back, revealing that he was carrying a large wild goose.

"Oh Uncle Li, you shouldn't have," Seeing Li Mutian bring a gift, Tian Yun was momentarily taken aback. 

Upon closer inspection, she exclaimed in surprise, "Is that a wild goose?"

The ⟦Book of Etiquette and Ceremonial on the Marriage Rites of the Ordinary Officer⟧ states: For the marriage rites, when the proposal is sent down and accepted, employ a wild goose.

In the Yue State, when a man's family wished to arrange a marriage with a woman's family, they would bring a wild goose to the door. This ritual was known as the formal proposal.

Small farming households could not adhere to overly complex rules. For an ordinary family in Lijing Village, let alone a wild goose, there were plenty who came empty-handed to propose. This was also Tian Yun's first time seeing a formal proposal, taking her a moment to realize what was happening.

"That Xiangping of my family, do you fancy him?"

Li Mutian laughed heartily, feeling the urge to tease her.

Tian Yun had already flushed red from the tips of her ears down to her neck. Out of modesty and shyness, she stammered, wanting to gloss over it. Yet, afraid that Li Mutian might take her evasion seriously, she had no choice but to say, "I do!"

Inside the house, Tian Shoushui had just stepped out the main door. Hearing this, he let out a massive sigh of relief; the stone that had long weighed upon his heart finally fell to the ground.

As a father, how could he not see that his daughter's heart had long been hitched to that Li Xiangping!

It was just that the boy was usually airtight in his demeanor, making it impossible for anyone to read his thoughts. Tian Shoushui feared his daughter would end up embarrassed, so he had remained completely silent on the matter.

"Eldest Brother!" Tian Shoushui called out with a loud laugh.

Tian Shoushui had followed behind Li Mutian since he was five years old. At the age of twelve, he had even enlisted in the army alongside Ren Ping'an. The three of them had banded together to survive in the military; they were not blood brothers, but their bond surpassed even that.

Later, after returning to the village, they slaughtered the wealthy Yuan family, washed their hands of the bloody past, and took up farming. Li Mutian had looked after him in every respect—distributing land to him and even acting as a matchmaker so he could take a wife.

He, Tian Shoushui, shared an inseparable bond of fortune and misfortune with the Li family. He had even personally helped raise Li Changhu, viewing him more dearly than his own child.

With his own daughter marrying into their family, how could she possibly suffer any grievances?

Tian Yun, entirely unaware of the numerous twists and turns in her father's thoughts, retreated hastily the momemt she saw him emerge, drawing another burst of laughter from Li Mutian.

"Eldest Brother, aside from this marriage, there is also an important matter," Tian Shoushui said, shaking his head helplessly, his expression turning serious.

"What is it?"

Li Mutian looked up, somewhat puzzled.

"A few days ago, when I was heading up the mountain, I passed by the Yuan family's ancestral tomb and heard some commotion. Yet, when I turned back to look, I saw absolutely nothing."

"Are there still surviving clansmen of the Yuan family that we failed to kill?" Li Mutian raised an eyebrow, his tone turning gloomy.

"I suspect it is merely some distant relatives or connections who dared to sneak in and offer sacrifices." Tian Shoushui felt an uneasy flutter in his heart and quickly tried to console himself.

"Indeed, what you say makes sense." Li Mutian gradually relaxed and nodded at Tian Shoushui. "Back then, we thoroughly investigated it. There were five members of the Yuan family; there was no mistake or omission."

"Eldest Brother, on such a joyous day, I should not have brought up such unlucky words!" Tian Shoushui gave himself a light slap on the mouth, amusing Li Mutian enough to elicit a soft chuckle.

Stepping out of the Tian family's home, Li Mutian walked along the dirt path with a calm expression, gazing thoughtfully at the Great Li Mountains.

---

Having concluded the marriage discussions, Li Mutian hummed a cheerful tune as he returned to the courtyard. Crossing the front yard, he found only three brothers chatting idly at the wooden table in the central patio.

His youngest son, Li Chejing, was in his room cultivating with his eyes closed. Although lunar essence was thin during the day, making progress slow, Li Chejing cultivated diligently day and night, refusing to waste even a sliver of time.

In the courtyard, Li Xiangping was repeatedly poring over the ⟨Reception Method⟩. The silk cloth had been thoroughly manhandled, covered in creases, with the ink smudging considerably.

"Third Brother, be a bit gentler with your tormenting," Li Tongya chided lightly with a smile, though the carving knife in his hand never ceased as he silently inscribed characters onto wooden slips.

The eldest brother, Li Changhu, was off to the side carefully calculating accounts against the land deeds.

Hearing this, he raised an eyebrow and said, "He has been tormenting it all morning."

Li Mutian stepped over the stone threshold, picked up a cup of clear tea from the wooden table, and sat right down on the steps, saying nonchalantly, "I went to the Tian family to propose marriage."

At those words, Li Xiangping leaped straight up from his chair, staring at his father with a face full of anxious urgency.

"What did the Tian family say?"

"Tian Yun said she fancies you." Li Mutian took a slow sip of his tea and let out a comfortable sigh.

"Good, good, that is wonderful." Li Xiangping nodded in excitement.

"Hahahahaha..." His two older brothers could not help but laugh.

"Listen here, all of you." Li Mutian, however, was thinking of other matters. He set down his teacup and adopted a stern tone.

"Though the path of immortality is said to be unfathomably wondrous, who knows what terrors and perils lie in wait? The Li family's numbers are already thin. If something happens to you, who will carry on the Li family legacy?"

Seeing the young men turn serious, Li Mutian waved his hand and continued, "This dharma mirror is our family's fortuitous encounter, but it could also be our family's ruin."

"I hastily arranged a marriage for Changhu and held the wedding with the hope that he would quickly produce an heir. It is precisely because I fear that, should a disaster occur, our Li family might still possess a sliver of hope for survival."

"The same goes for you." Li Mutian pointed at Li Xiangping, then looked at Li Tongya and sighed helplessly.

"As for Tongya... I am old, and I can no longer sway you. You have no interest in the women of this village, but you must still leave behind an heir..."

Li Tongya nodded in silence. It took a few breaths before he finally spoke, "Rest assured, Father. I know the measure of things."

"That is for the best."

Li Mutian brushed a hand over the white hair at his temples, his heart swirling with anxiety.

He was nearly sixty years old. Though his body remained robust and he had no worries over food and clothing, the affairs of the world were impermanent. It was better to arrange the family's matters as early as possible!

"Father!"

A crisp, clear shout interrupted the somewhat oppressive atmosphere in the courtyard. Li Chejing dashed out of the house and stood firmly before his older brothers.

"I am about to finish refining eighty-one wisps of lunar essence and condense the Profound Scenery Wheel!" Li Chejing raised his head with pride.

"My Chejing is truly formidable."

Li Mutian had long heard his youngest son babble endlessly about the gateway to the Fetal Breathing realm and the Profound Scenery Wheel. Overjoyed, he pulled him into a hug and laughed heartily as he looked at the child.

The brothers were also infected by the joyous atmosphere, smiles breaking out on their faces. Li Changhu even pinched Li Chejing's cheeks, not letting go until the boy cried out in repeated complaint.

"The next time we can receive the talisman seeds will not be until the Summer Solstice."

Li Tongya smiled as he watched the joyful scene in the courtyard, pondering to himself, _We missed the days of the dark and new moons because we were ill-prepared. The next occasion is the Summer Solstice; we merely have to wait a few more days._

He quietly continued to carve the ⟨Reception Method⟩ onto the wooden slips, yet his heart surged with tempestuous waves of unbridled excitement.

_The path of immortal cultivation—Li Tongya has arrived._

[^1]: In traditional Chinese culture, setting up an altar with burning incense and offerings of fruit is the standard ritual for ancestral veneration—specifically, making offerings to the deceased. Because Lu Jiangxian's soul is currently trapped inside the mirror resting on the altar, watching the Li family bow and burn incense to it makes him feel as though he is attending his own funeral or memorial service.
[^2]: **Niwan Palace (泥丸宫 - Níwán Gōng)**

    Literally translating to the "Mud Pellet Palace," this is a fundamental concept in Daoist internal alchemy (Neidan). It refers to the Upper Dantian, located within the brain (often associated with the pineal gland). In cultivation literature, the Niwan Palace is considered the seat of a person's consciousness, soul, and spiritual energy (Shen)
[^3]: **Qihai aperture (气海穴 - Qìhǎi Xué)**

    Literally translating to the "Sea of Qi."
    
    It is located in the lower abdomen, roughly two finger-widths below the navel.
[^4]: **Twelve-Story Pagoda (十二重楼 - Shí'èr Chónglóu)**

    In Daoist texts, the "Twelve-Story Pagoda" is a metaphorical, anatomical term referring to the human windpipe or throat area.
