# Chapter 42: Reinforcements

_The flesh and blood of this two-legged beast king is the most fragrant; I absolutely cannot let him slip away._

It narrowed its eyes, watching the two-legged beasts in the distance hurriedly gather together, pointing those brightly burning fires at it.

It chuckled coldly in its heart.

_I will first exhaust their stamina. Once these two-legged beasts scatter in a panic, I can devour that beast king._

It cautiously and slowly accelerated, weaving through the snow-covered forest. Seeing those two-legged beasts become increasingly panicked and their formation increasingly scattered, it was extremely satisfied.

Ever since it ate that injured white deer, its mind had become increasingly clear. Relying on this ever-clearer mind, it had dodged several encirclements by the patterned two-legged beasts, ultimately abandoning its own pack and stumbling its way to this place.

It had dealt with many of these two-legged beasts and knew well that this kind of two-legged beast king often spat black mist and threw deep green bone swords; pressing them too tightly would only result in injury to itself.

It needed to press them slowly, stepping forward to attack from time to time, exhausting the two-legged beasts' methods bit by bit before safely devouring them.

_This kind of two-legged beast has no patterns on its body; I do not know what breed it is._

It carelessly leapt over a sweeping long club, lowered its head to dodge a flying arrow, and bit down on the arm of a two-legged beast, crushing it to pieces.

It thought viciously in its heart, _If my children had not all been slaughtered by that patterned two-legged beast, why would I need to act personally!_

Recalling being chased across seven great mountains by that patterned two-legged beast, the vital energy and dharma power it had damaged had yet to recover to this day, bringing a bit more coldness to its jade-green eyes.

This unpatterned two-legged beast seemed much weaker. The golden light it released lacked power but was continuous and infuriating.

---

The winter mountain forest was particularly quiet at night. Thick snow had accumulated in the woods; as people brushed past, one could hear the rustling of snow continuously falling, occasionally punctuated by a creak as the heavy snow snapped a tree branch.

"Swish!"

Li Xiangping shot a glittering golden arrow. Watching the wolf lower its head to dodge and crush a village guard's arm, he drew his bow and fired again without a change in expression, forcing back the giant wolf that was preparing to pounce on the next village guard.

Watching the village guard spray blood and fall to the ground screaming, painting a scorching flower of blood upon the pristine white snow, the other guards could not help but shiver. They swung the long clubs in their hands weakly. Beside them, Chen Erniu picked up the man with a cold expression, haphazardly wrapped some clothing around the wound, hoisted him onto his shoulder, and hurried to catch up with the group.

"How much further to Mount Lijing?!" Li Xiangping shouted in a low voice, trudging unevenly through the snow as he called out loudly to Chen Erniu.

Chen Erniu instantly understood his intent. Handing the village guard on his back to someone else, he looked into the distance and answered loudly, "Another quarter of an hour!"

Hearing that they were gradually approaching Mount Lijing, the village guards felt their hearts settle considerably. Gnashing their teeth, they glared at the demon, praying in their hearts that they would not be the next one pinned to the ground.

Li Xiangping wiped the fresh blood from his face. The dharma power in his dantian was nearly depleted. Looking at the terrified and trembling village guards beside him, he bellowed in a deep voice, "We only need to lure this demon into the mountains to ambush and kill it! If we cannot kill this demon today, then we might as well let it kill us here and now. You, you, and you—the women and children in your homes will all become bloody meals for this demon's maw!"

As soon as these words were spoken, the village guards looked at one another, their expressions firming considerably. One by one, they raised their arms, heavy as lead, and pointed their torches and long clubs back at the demon.

"Awoo—"

The demon licked the fresh blood from its silver-gray claws. Looking at the torches and long clubs pointed at it, it seemed somewhat impatient. It stomped the ground, accelerated its pouncing speed, and consecutively bit down on several village guards.

"Crack!"

A whip crack exploded out of thin air, and a jade-green rattan whip shimmering with a lustrous glow lashed out straight toward it. Caught off guard, the demon's waist was instantly torn open, leaving a bloody gash.

"Awoo—"

The demon let out a high-pitched howl and retreated several steps, viciously glaring at the brocade-clad man holding the long whip.

"Patriarch!"

Upon seeing the rattan whip, Wan Tiancang immediately let out a long breath. A heavy stone in his heart settled, and a look of joy appeared on his face.

Wan Xiaohua succeeded in his strike but did not pursue. His face went pale for a moment, and he stood in place adjusting his breath for a few cycles before color slowly returned to his cheeks.

Upon receiving Wan Tiancang's handwritten letter, Wan Xiaohua consecutively cast three ⟨Divine Movement Arts⟩ to rush from the Wan family estate to Lichuankou, and then used that dharma artifact to repel the demon. Even with his cultivation at the peak of the Jade Capital Wheel, he was somewhat short of breath, needing to regulate his breathing for a few breaths to recover his stamina.

Wan Xiaohua looked at the shallow wound on the demon's back and could not help but inhale a breath of cold air, speaking with an unsightly expression, "What a tough demon."

"Mortals, leave first."

His rattan whip was originally a Qi Refinement stage dharma artifact. With his cultivation at the peak of the Jade Capital Wheel, using it consumed a great deal of energy, but it was enough to split monuments and shatter rocks. Yet, it only left a bloody mark on the demon's back. Although it was a hasty strike, it was enough to show the immense hardness of this demon's body.

Li Xiangping waved his hand, and Chen Erniu hurriedly directed the village guards to carry the wounded and trudge through the snow westward toward Lijing.

Looking at the man and beast confronting each other in the snow, Li Xiangping called out loudly, "Patriarch Wan, if this demon proves too difficult to defeat, flee towards the southwest. There is an array formation on Mount Lijing to protect us. Even if we cannot deal with this demon, you can escape with your life!"

Wan Xiaohua cast a low glance at Li Xiangping and replied in a low voice, "Understood."

He raised the long whip in his hand, and it whistled through the air, lashing toward the demon's waist.

"Awoo—"

The demon gave its waist a slight twist, dodging the rattan whip, and closed the distance with Wan Xiaohua in two bounds, lunging straight for him.

A foul wind rushed at his face, yet Wan Xiaohua remained calm and composed. His fingers formed a dharma technique, and a small white-jade shield materialized out of thin air. Although it shattered under a single strike from the demon's paw, Wan Xiaohua used the opportunity to widen the distance between them.

The demon tapped the ground lightly and lunged at Wan Xiaohua once again. 

Wan Xiaohua's eyes lit up, and he thought to himself, _A perfect opportunity!_

The rattan whip in his hand suddenly burst with white light, lashing toward the wolf's waist. Yet the demon neither hid nor dodged. A trace of cunning flashed through its eyes as its wolf maw opened wide, churning out bursts of pitch-black breath that shot straight for Wan Xiaohua's face.

_Curses! What a cunning beast!_

Wan Xiaohua cursed inwardly, forced to release the rattan whip in his hand and retreat continuously.

The demon forcibly endured Wan Xiaohua's whip strike, letting out a low growl. It used its claws to dig the rattan whip out of the ground, stepped on it, and stared at Wan Xiaohua, its jade-green eyes watching him coldly.

"Such a cunning demon..."

Wan Xiaohua looked at this giant silver-gray wolf, a bitter smile hooking the corner of his mouth. His Wan family did not border the Great Li Mountains and rarely had experience fighting demons, much less a Jade Capital Wheel demon like this one.

The demon kicked back with its hind claws, burying the rattan whip in the snow, and bounded toward Wan Xiaohua over the shallow snow. He hurriedly formed a seal to cast a dharma technique, and the small white-jade shield materialized before him once more.

However, black qi surged between the demon's four paws as it leapt into the air, smashing the small white-jade shield to pieces in a single blow, its wolf maw biting toward Wan Xiaohua's neck.
