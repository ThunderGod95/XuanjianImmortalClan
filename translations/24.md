# Chapter 24: Returning to the Clan

"Excellent! To think there is yet another, and of my Li family's bloodline at that!"

Hearing Li Tongya's gentle voice from the stage, Ye Changfu leaped up from the ground as if struck by lightning. He gazed in profound excitement at the bewildered Ye Qiuyang on the stage, itching to throw his head back and laugh out loud.

"Since your surname is Ye, who is your father?" Li Tongya asked with a smile.

Ye Qiuyang looked at Ye Changfu, who was dancing with joy below the stage, and answered timidly, "Ye... Ye Changfu."

Li Tongya followed the child's gaze downward and offered a smiling greeting, "Congratulations, Brother Changfu."

"Many thanks to the immortal master!"

Ye Changfu did not dare to act presumptuously, behaving with the utmost politeness, though the corners of his mouth twitched upward uncontrollably. He excitedly cupped his hands and replied with profound respect.

Li Tongya smiled and shook his head, testing the remaining few one by one before announcing loudly, "The rest of you lack the destiny. The stewards shall remain; everyone else may return to your villages."

The crowd below stirred for a moment, sighs of regret rising and falling in succession. After a cup of tea's time[^1] of commotion, they departed the village in their respective directions.

Beside Li Tongya, Li Yesheng stepped forward and spoke, "You three stewards, follow me to the Li family courtyard so we may balance the land deeds and the ledgers."

Chen Erniu and the other two naturally voiced their collective agreement, following Li Yesheng toward the Li estate.

Li Tongya descended from the stage. Looking at the endlessly agitated Ye Changfu and the elder of the Jingyang Liu clan, he said softly, "Follow me, if you please."

Ye Changfu nodded hastily. As he walked, he pulled his child tightly into his embrace, his face suddenly streaming with tears, only murmuring in a low voice, "Good, good."

---

Ye Changfu and Ye Qiuyang offered incense in the ancestral hall, kneeling to kowtow with the utmost reverence. Under the witness of the various Li family members, they returned to the clan surname.

"Patriarch!"

As the eldest direct descendant of the Li clan, Li Mutian naturally sat high in the seat of honor within the courtyard. In accordance with the rules, the two first offered tea to Li Mutian, then knelt before him to respectfully receive his words.

"Qiuyang is also a scion of my Li clan. Since he possesses a spiritual aperture, it is only natural that we pass down our methods to him."

Li Mutian took a wooden slip in his hand and continued softly, "The clan bestows upon you the ⟨Azure Essence Wheel-Nurturing Method⟩. You must cultivate diligently, revere the ancestors and honor the Dao, and foster harmony within the clan. You must absolutely never leak the secret essence of this immortal art, lest you draw the covetous eyes of others."

The young boy, who was now Li Qiuyang, bowed deeply. With a resolute gaze, he answered, "I will firmly remember the Patriarch's teachings."

"You shall follow Xiangping to the spirit fields in Jingyang. Train under him daily, and until you have cultivated the Profound Scenery Wheel, you are not permitted to leave."

Li Mutian nodded, laying out the arrangements.

"Yes!"

Li Qiuyang rose and accepted the wooden slip from his hands. Being only an eight or nine-year-old child after all, he turned around with a hint of a sob in his voice, choking back tears as he spoke to his father, Li Changfu, "Father, this child will now go to cultivate with the main family."

"Good, good! You absolutely must not worry about matters at home. Tomorrow, your father will shoulder your clothes and utensils and deliver them to your courtyard in Jingyang. Just focus on your cultivation. You just focus on your cultivation..." Li Changfu nodded repeatedly, instructing him with reddened eyes.

"Come, Qiuyang."

Li Xiangping offered a gentle smile. He took Li Qiuyang's small hand, nodded to his father Li Mutian, and led the boy out the door.

Li Mutian coughed and spoke with a frown, "Changfu, go and pack your belongings as well. In a few days, move your family to the back of the village."

Li Changfu gave a flurry of assents. Noting the expressions of the two men, he quickly took his leave.

Seeing Li Changfu hurriedly walk out the door and fade into the distance, Li Mutian said in a deep voice, "Take the girl from the Liu family with you for now and instruct her in the Lijing courtyard. If the day comes when she achieves the Profound Scenery Wheel, do not impart any arts to her at first."

"A brilliant arrangement, Father. The Liu clan's Jingyang will be overseen by our Li family, while the Liu clan's cultivator will be transferred to Lijing to be watched. This will prevent them from growing too powerful."

Li Tongya gasped in realization, speaking with boundless admiration.

"It is still early," Li Mutian narrowed his eyes, took a light sip of clear tea, and continued, "These children have no mirror to assist them, nor any cultivation resources or medicinal pills. Will it take them a year to cultivate the Profound Scenery Wheel?"

"Far more than that!" Li Tongya shook his head, explaining, "I read through the Azure Essence Wheel-Nurturing Method. The dharma power cultivated through it is likely frail and lacks strength, and there are many burdens during its circulation. It will take more than three years to condense the Profound Scenery Wheel."

"That is it, then. Since the Azure Essence Wheel-Nurturing Method is inferior in both achievement and speed, there is fundamentally no need to guard against anything. It is simply keeping a card up our sleeve, as one should in all things."

"Father speaks truly."

Li Tongya nodded, gently setting down the tea in his hand. Seeing Li Mutian gazing into the distance as if lost in a trance, he gave a bitter smile, cupped his hands, and withdrew.

Li Mutian sat alone and in silence upon the high seat in the courtyard. Only after a long while did he utter a soft sigh.

"Not ruthless enough..."

---

Xu Wenshan had been managing Lidaokou for over four months. He had dispatched his several aides, and the village men had been ruled into complete submission.

Xu Wenshan originally came from a background of managing merchant caravans; commanding a tiny village was naturally a trivial matter for him. Lidaokou was a mixture of various surnames. Though the situation was complex, it also lacked the constraints of large, dominant clans. Within a single month, Xu Wenshan had thoroughly grasped control of the village from top to bottom.

However, with his newfound idle time, Xu Wenshan began pondering how to establish merit before the main family to overshadow Chen Erniu. Merely sitting in this village collecting rent and balancing ledgers was truly beneath a man of Xu Wenshan's caliber.

Turning over the map in his hands out of sheer boredom, Xu Wenshan stared blankly into space.

Speaking of Chen Erniu, there had always been constant friction between the two villages regarding the division of fields and water sources.

Xu Wenshan was naturally more than happy to battle it out with him.

It was plainly evident now—whenever the villagers of Lidaokou spoke of Chen Erniu's Lichuankou, they gnashed their teeth, itching to land a punch on his face.

_Only with an enemy will they rally closely around me, Xu Wenshan!_ he thought smugly.

Furthermore, during his days managing caravans, Xu Wenshan never liked his subordinates becoming too friendly with one another; he always had to stir up some conflicts.

If someone claimed the Li family had no informants in these villages, Xu Wenshan would absolutely not believe it. Judging others by his own standards, Xu Wenshan naturally had to keep fighting with Chen Erniu to keep the main family pleased with what they saw.

"I have already won far too much against that Chen Erniu."

He muttered to himself. His thoughts returned to the map laid out before him. Looking at the crooked, winding lines of the Guli Road on the map, his heart suddenly gave a jolt.

_Why don't I return to my old trade?_

He slapped his thigh, thinking with delighted surprise, _The villages are trapped within this Guli Road, where goods cannot circulate. Every household plows the fields and weaves clothes, utterly self-sufficient. I, Xu Wenshan, worked the trade routes for over twenty years; why couldn't I work this road?_

_The main family cultivates the immortal Dao, making these mortal goods dispensable to them. It would be better to open up the mountain path and sell all this grain and silk to the outside. Perhaps we could exchange them for jade and spiritual artifacts; that is what would truly be of use._

At this thought, Xu Wenshan truly could not sit still any longer. He called over the several aides who had fled from the disaster with him and laid out his plan. The men immediately clapped their hands and cheered in approval.

"I shall go to the main family right now to explain this in detail!"

Xu Wenshan laughed uproariously, leading his men out the door.

[^1]: A traditional Chinese cultural measurement of time frequently used in classical literature and the Xianxia genre. It refers to the time it takes to brew and drink a hot cup of tea, generally understood to be roughly 10 to 15 minutes.
