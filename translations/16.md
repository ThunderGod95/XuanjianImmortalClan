# Chapter 16: Ill-Fate (I)

Li Changhu only saw the youth throw aside the animal hide, revealing a glint of cold steel. Those brutal, razor-sharp eyes, akin to a wild beast's, did not even blink as they stared back at Li Changhu with vindictive glee and ferocity.

The cold steel struck as swift as lightning, thrusting straight toward Li Changhu's throat. In his terror, he only had time to retreat a single step and hastily throw up a hand to block.

It was too fast!

This stab had been practiced for twenty-two years. How many straw dummies had been pierced to shreds? How many nights of hatred had been suppressed?

That cold gleam was clearly a streak of bright silver lightning, yet with the agility of a swimming fish, it slipped past Li Changhu's blocking hand and plunged straight into his throat.

Li Changhu could only let out a few blood-choked whimpers before crashing heavily to the ground. His vision blurred entirely, leaving him to faintly hear the screams and furious roars erupting around him.

"You beast!!!"

Tian Shoushui, who had been standing behind Li Changhu, unleashed a heart-wrenching roar. Being shorter than Li Changhu, his view had been completely obscured; it was only when Li Changhu fell that he realized what had happened.

His eyes bulged with bloodshot rage, and veins bulged visibly across his head. He lunged forward to grab the youth.

Having landed his strike, the youth abandoned the dagger. Capitalizing on the chaos as everyone rushed to aid Li Changhu, he rolled on the ground to evade Tian Shoushui's grasp. Like a nimble deer, he crashed headlong into the throng of refugees.

Tian Shoushui strode in pursuit. The refugees scattered like a disturbed nest of bees, parting in all directions. He only managed to catch a glimpse of the youth diving into the reed marshes by the village's edge, fleeing swiftly into the distance.

"Son of a bitch."

Without looking back, Tian Shoushui plunged into the reed marshes, tracking the trail and leaving behind the terrified, huddled mass of refugees.

"Curse him! That son of a dog!"

As the crowd finally reacted, the villagers viciously pinned the middle-aged man into a corner. He wailed and cursed, "It truly has nothing to do with this lowly one!! I don't know that young man! He sneaked in along the way!"

"Shut up!"

Liu Linfeng spat, his face pale and ghastly. Gnashing his teeth, he held the fallen Li Changhu and shot the middle-aged man a venomous glare, having completely lost his composure.

Seeing his nephew coughing up bloody froth, clearly beyond saving, his mind devolved into utter chaos. How was he supposed to explain this to his sister? How could he answer to Li Mutian? His nephew had died pointlessly right before his very eyes!

Ren Ping'an sat blankly on the ground. His daughter, Lady Ren, had just married Li Changhu and had only recently fallen pregnant, and now Li Changhu was dead. The boy he had watched grow up was just dead!

While everyone panicked, Li Changhu simply lay on the ground, his open eyes gazing at the sky. He felt the scalding blood pooling across his chest as his consciousness gradually slipped away.

_I am just sorry to Xiangping, for turning his joyous affair into a mourning one,_ Li Changhu's fading consciousness thought bleakly. The pain and suffocation finally plunged him into boundless darkness.

Amidst the chaos, unnoticed by the crowd, a streak of silver light quietly rose from his dantian. Trailing a tail of light, it shot into the sky like a young swallow returning to the nest, twirled once in the air, and flew straight toward the Li family's rear courtyard.

Not far away.

Old Man Xu stood rooted to the spot, his hunched body trembling uncontrollably. Watching the youth plunge the knife into Li Changhu's neck—the tip of the blade even faintly protruding from the back of his neck—he felt a surge of bloody rush from his chest straight to his brain. His vision darkened in waves, and he nearly fainted on the spot.

"What an Ill-fate!"

Old tears streamed down his face. Clutching the string of grass crickets he had meant to bring for Li Changhu in a death grip, he pressed a hand to his heart in agony.

"Beast..."

Old Man Xu took a deep, ragged breath and wiped the tears from his face. He broke into a run toward the rear mountain, muttering incessantly, "This old man outlived his wife and watched his master's family burn. I've seen more men of note than a wild wolf like you has taken shits! Just you wait! Just you wait! This old man will deal with you!"

---

The Li Family Residence.

Inside the ancestral hall, Lu Jiangxian sighed in silence. He had awoken the moment Li Changhu was assassinated, but the village entrance was simply too far away. He was utterly powerless to intervene, forced to watch helplessly as it all unfolded.

Gazing at the silver light fluttering around the edge of the mirror like a butterfly, he knew that with a single thought, this talisman seed could be converted into pure essence to nourish his soul.

However, because Li Changhu had never cultivated to the Profound Scenery stage, the benefits provided by the talisman seed would be minuscule. With a sigh, Lu Jiangxian reversed his incantations. Utilizing the Profound Pearl Spirit-Sacrificing Art, he catalyzed the spiritual sense lingering within the talisman seed. Streams of dharma power, extracted from the talisman seed, materialized in the courtyard and extended out toward the members of the Li family.

Li Mutian had been lying in bed for over an hour before finally drifting into a groggy sleep. Suddenly, he saw moonlight cascading through the windows and the cracks of the door like flowing water, illuminating the outside as bright as day. His eldest son, Li Changhu, was standing by the bed, gazing at him quietly.

"What happened?"

Li Mutian asked his eldest son in a hoarse voice.

Li Changhu kept his lips pressed tightly together and said nothing. He stared carefully at Li Mutian's face, as if trying to memorize his features for eternity.

"Cough, cough."

Li Mutian coughed heavily, furrowing his brow as he asked again, "Changhu, what happened?"

Instead, Li Changhu bowed deeply and respectfully to his father. Choking on his words, he finally spoke, "Father, take care of yourself..."

The moment the words left his lips, Li Changhu's figure scattered into the wind like drifting grains of sand. The water-like moonlight slowly dimmed, and the courtyard, previously bright as day, plunged into darkness once more.

Gripped by intense unease, Li Mutian reached out his hand to grasp the air, crying out incessantly.

"Child, speak to me! Changhu! Li Changhu!"

He jolted violently awake from his bed, staring blankly out the window.

---

The Side Wing.

_Can one still dream while cultivating?_

In the side wing, Li Chejing opened his eyes, his face streaked with tears. He got up and walked out of the room, only to see Li Tongya sitting by the wooden table.

Seeing his younger brother come out, Li Tongya quickly wiped the tear tracks from his own face and forced a smile. "What's the matter?"

Li Chejing merely stared at his elder brother blankly before lifting his head to ask, "Where is Eldest Brother?"

"Eldest Brother was called away early by a tenant. There's likely some matter to attend to in the fields." Li Tongya smiled and replied.

Just then, the door creaked open. Li Mutian stood in the doorway, his face etched with profound dread as he called out, "Tongya! Go and call your Eldest Brother back... Never mind, I will go myself."

Li Mutian hurriedly slipped on his shoes and walked out to the front courtyard. Pushing open the courtyard gates, he froze in shock.

Outside stood a large circle of villagers, all carrying torches and weeping silently. Three figures knelt at the base of the stone steps, and laid out before them was something covered by a white cloth.

Seeing the ashamed and grief-stricken expressions on the faces of Tian Shoushui and Ren Ping'an, the dread in Li Mutian's heart reached its absolute peak. With trembling hands, he walked down the steps. Pushing aside a tearful Liu Linfeng who had stepped forward to support him, he gently lifted the white cloth.

Li Mutian's vision went black, nearly causing him to faint. His sons, who had followed him out, also wore deeply unsettled expressions. Li Chejing stepped forward to look, and with a heavy thud, collapsed to his knees, crying out in anguish, "Brother!"

Throwing himself over Li Changhu's body to check for breath, Li Chejing abruptly lifted his head. Sobbing, with tears streaming down his face, he glared at the surrounding villagers and roared fiercely, "Who! Who was it?! Who would dare?"

This furious roar echoed like the roar of a tiger, frightening the crowd so badly that they all stumbled back a few steps, staring at him in sheer trembling terror.
