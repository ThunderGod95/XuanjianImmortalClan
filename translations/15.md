# Chapter 15: Refugees

Lu Jiangxian had spent several months familiarizing himself with his new state and had completely absorbed the power of the jade. He had long been capable of executing the minor illusion arts recorded in the ⟨Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture⟩ to communicate with the Li family.

He reasoned, however, that the time was not yet ripe. Thus, he had never revealed his sentience, opting instead to lie quietly in the ancestral hall like a lifeless object.

After all, the Li family was by no means foolish. Perhaps this dharma mirror possessed a profoundly high status, but Lu Jiangxian himself was, in truth, a complete novice in the ways of cultivation.

To speak now would require him not only to fabricate an origin story but also to answer the myriad questions brewing in the Li family's minds. It would only expose him to unnecessary flaws and suspicions.

Fortunately, his divine sense was now more than sufficient to envelop the entire Li family compound. He spent his days sleeping, and upon waking, he treated their lives like a grand theatrical play—a rather leisurely existence.

Only the occasional auras flying through the skies above Guli Road would jolt Lu Jiangxian awake. Heart clenching in alarm, he would sense those auras—some overwhelmingly majestic, others ethereal and light—and silently retract his divine sense into a tight ball.

Although the Li family had envisioned Lu Jiangxian as some immortal artifact, he had a clear grasp of his own combat prowess. At the very least, he was comparable to the second level of Fetal Breathing, the Bearing Brightness Wheel, and at the absolute maximum, the fourth level, the Azure Essence Wheel.

Comparing himself to those majestic auras that made him feel as if needles were pricking his back even while hiding within the mirror, he decided it was best to remain cautious. He would lay low for another eighty to a hundred years before doing anything else.

"It's just... why are there refugees arriving on Guli Road..." he murmured to himself in confusion.

---

Li Changhu led a group of villagers bearing torches, pitchforks, hoes, and other implements. They stood at the village entrance, confronting a throng of ragged refugees.

Having just finished the preparations for Xiangping and Tian Yun's wedding over the past few days, Li Changhu had finally found time to cultivate. Yet, a tenant of the Li family had hurriedly come to report that a group of refugees had arrived at the village entrance.

"Refugees?"

The last time Li Changhu had seen refugees was more than three years ago—a family surnamed Chen who had crossed over from Meichi Mountain. The weather had been favorable these past few years, and the Meichi River nourished the land well. There were hardly any families struggling to survive along its upper and lower reaches.

"They claim to have come from Guli Road," the tenant replied respectfully, seeming to have found his backbone upon seeing Li Changhu.

"How is that possible..."

Li Changhu considered the matter for a few breaths. Waving his hand, he stepped out the door and said, "Father has already retired for the night. There is no need to trouble him. Call for Uncle Tian and Uncle Ren; we will go take a look."

By the time Li Changhu reached the village entrance, Liu Linfeng, the head of the Liu family—the other prominent household in Lijing Village—was already waiting there, clenching a dry tobacco pipe between his teeth.

Seeing Li Changhu, he smiled and said, "Changhu, you've arrived."

"Eldest Uncle."

Li Changhu nodded in greeting. Liu Linfeng was the biological elder brother of his mother, Liu Linyun. Back when Li Mutian had killed the wealthy Yuan family and distributed their lands, Liu Linfeng's father had immediately taken a liking to the young man. Overcoming all objections, he married Liu Linyun off to him, thus allying with the Li family.

Li Changhu and Liu Linfeng led their men and called out to the refugees three times before the crowd finally pushed forward a middle-aged man. Though his face was smeared with soot and his clothes were in tatters, his movements and gestures carried a distinct air of dignity.

The middle-aged man cupped his hands in greeting and looked at the two with a bitter smile. "This humble one was originally the manager of a merchant caravan on Xiali Road. The Wu State to the south has conquered Jingxia City, and the entirety of Xiali Road[^1] is now engulfed in the chaos of war, leaving neither man nor beast in peace. We were robbed on our journey and managed to escape by blending in with the refugees. Untalented as I am, the crowd has nominated me to speak on their behalf. I can only hope you two might take us in."

"This section of Guli Road has long been in disrepair, and wild beasts run rampant. You managed to cross it just like that?" Liu Linfeng asked in confusion.

"Naturally, many died. The elderly and the children have all perished," the middle-aged man said with a bitter smile.

While the representatives of the two prominent families inquired about their backgrounds, Old Man Xu stood in the crowd, holding woven grass crickets in one hand and a hoe in the other, squinting as he evaluated the refugees.

He lived at the village entrance and had been roused early by the commotion. Upon hearing that Li Changhu was also coming, Old Man Xu had gleefully retrieved the grass crickets he had woven a few days prior, intending to give them to the unborn child in Lady Ren's womb.

Presently, however, his attention was drawn to a young man among the refugees. The youth wore tattered clothes and had a piece of animal hide wrapped around his waist. His eyes, burning as if filled with fire, were fixed intently upon Li Changhu and Liu Linfeng at the front.

"These eyes look so familiar," Old Man Xu murmured, stroking his pale beard, yet he could not recall where he had seen them before.

"Everyone, listen to me!" Liu Linfeng called out to settle the refugees. Taking a step forward, he shouted, "I am the patriarch of the Liu family in this village. Lijing Village still has a vast amount of wasteland available for reclamation. If you are willing, the Liu family can provide you with food and tools for this year. The reclaimed land will be considered rented to you by our Liu family, and we will only collect a thirty percent rent."

As the junior, Li Changhu stood half a step behind Liu Linfeng and made the same promise. "My Li family offers the same."

Upon hearing this, the young refugee whipped his head around, his gaze locking dead onto Li Changhu's face. Those fire-lit eyes stared intently for several seconds before he swiftly lowered his head in satisfaction.

Old Man Xu expended a tremendous amount of effort to squeeze his way to the front of the crowd, scrutinizing the refugees closely, but he could no longer find the young man.

Turning his head, he realized the youth had already pushed his way to the very front of the refugees, now standing only a few paces away from where Li Changhu and Liu Linfeng stood.

The middle-aged man, standing respectfully beside the two patriarchs, cast a glance at the youth and wondered in surprise, _Such sharp eyes. To think there is someone of this caliber among the refugees? Having eaten and lived together for a full three months, I have never actually seen this person._

"You may drink wine, eat meat, marry, and have children in Lijing Village. However, you must absolutely not steal or commit acts of depravity..." While Liu Linfeng was admonishing the crowd, the unease in Old Man Xu's heart grew increasingly intense. He watched as the young man suddenly threw himself forward, collapsing to his knees before Liu Linfeng and weeping with heavy sobs.

"This lowly one's entire family suffered the ravages of war; I am the only one to have escaped! I trekked a thousand miles before meeting you two great lords who are willing to take us in. I am endlessly grateful! How could I ever dare to commit such shameless acts here!"

He wept so sorrowfully that it stirred the tragic memories of the refugees behind him, causing them to break into loud wails. For a time, cries of grief echoed everywhere, and even Liu Linfeng could not help but be moved.

Old Man Xu, however, stared at the youth's exposed ankle as he knelt. His left foot, crisscrossed with scars, protruded from the tattered pant leg, and several black moles dotted his ankle joint.

An overwhelming sense of familiarity surged in his mind. His face flushed red as if he were drunk, and he stared vigilantly at the youth.

Behind him, Li Changhu could no longer bear it. Looking at the kneeling young man with pity, he stepped half a pace forward and bent down to help him up.

Old Man Xu's mind exploded with a resounding boom, his vision turning completely blank. It was as if he had instantly been transported back to that afternoon over twenty years ago.

He, Old Xu, was still a tenant for the Yuan family. The golden rice paddies undulated in the autumn wind. That woman had carried her child to the fields, and he had bowed low, uttering flattering, auspicious words, "Looking at these moles on the child's foot, he is destined to achieve great things!"

"Hold it right there!!" Old Man Xu forcefully straightened his back—a back that had been bowed for over twenty years. With his head held high and his eyes glaring, he bellowed in fury.

"Hold it right there!" At the same time, another voice echoed from nearby, shouting in perfect unison with him.

It turned out to be the middle-aged man nominated by the refugees. Staring at the youth's face in shock and suspicion, he had spoken out to stop him.

Before their voices could even fade, the youth jerked his head up. Caught completely off guard, Li Changhu met his gaze.

It was a pair of brutal, vicious eyes—eyes that belonged to a wild beast.
