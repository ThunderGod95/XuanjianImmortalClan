# Chapter 41: Wolf in the Mountains

Wan Tiancang had lived in Lichuankou for over half a year, staying indoors day after day. Watching the verdant green sprouts of spirit rice emerge from the soil one by one brought him immense joy. At night, he had cast the ⟨Spirit Rain Art⟩ over the spirit fields in his courtyard and was currently adjusting his breath in a cross-legged position, when he faintly heard a commotion of voices outside.

He could not help but open his eyes to look, seeing torchlight flickering in the deep gloom of the night outside the courtyard, as though people were running about holding torches. His heart immediately sank, and he opened the courtyard door to look outside.

Seeing a child running hurriedly toward him with a torch, Wan Tiancang quickly called out, "Child! What has happened?"

"My Lord! Old Ye at the edge of the village is dead. They say a demon has come, and they have called for the entire village to gather."

The child quickly halted his steps, holding up the torch as he looked at Wan Tiancang with apprehension. He watched as this immortal, who had not stepped out of his courtyard for over half a year, frowned, took half a step out the gate, and then pulled back.

"Of all places, a demon has struck Lichuankou! This spirit rice was planted barely half a year ago; absolutely nothing can be allowed to happen to it."

Wan Tiancang wanted to go find Li Tongya, yet he feared the demon might set its sights on the spirit rice in his garden. Caught in a dilemma, he had no choice but to crouch down and instruct the child, "Go and call Chen Erniu here."

"Yes!"

Scarcely had the child stood up when both of them saw torchlight flashing in the woods. A crowd of people emerged, closely surrounding Chen Erniu.

"Immortal Master! What are we to do!"

Chen Erniu was covered in sweat. The people from the main family had yet to arrive, and two more village guards had died in the village. Both had their brains dug out and died ghastly deaths. Chen Erniu was simultaneously terrified and enraged, pacing anxiously like an ant on a hot pan.

Wan Tiancang looked at the sweating Chen Erniu and asked with a stern, heavy voice, "Has anyone laid eyes on this demon?"

"No, the dead often perished without even uttering a single wail!"

Hearing this, Wan Tiancang immediately felt the urge to retreat. He was merely a minor cultivator at the peak of the Profound Scenery Wheel; where would he find the strength to deal with a demon that seemingly came and went like the wind?

He smiled bitterly in his heart, _Li Tongya, oh Li Tongya, where in the hell are you!_

Wan Tiancang paused for a few breaths. Just as he was about to speak, a loud shout approaching from the distance interrupted his words, "Brother Wan!"

He looked up to see a young man clad in rattan armor, holding a longbow, approaching with a spirited and imposing stride, flashing a bright, white-toothed smile at him.

"Young Patriarch!"

Chen Erniu hastily bowed in greeting. Wan Tiancang instantly understood the situation clearly; the man before him was undoubtedly Li Xiangping. 

He quickly cupped his hands and said, "Wan Tiancang of the Wan family greets the Young Patriarch."

Li Xiangping waved his hand, signaling Chen Erniu to rise, and addressed Wan Tiancang, "Wait a moment, Brother Wan."

He turned his head to look at Chen Erniu and spoke in a deep voice, "Have all the old, weak, women, and children of the village gather together around this courtyard. The village guards will form groups of ten to sit in an outer perimeter, thirty feet apart. Ensure that every group has at least five other groups within their line of sight to support one another."

Having said this, he entered the courtyard alongside Wan Tiancang, paying no mind to the others.

"Yes!"

Chen Erniu instantly felt he had found a pillar of support. He hurriedly agreed and immediately went to make the arrangements.

No sooner had Li Xiangping entered the courtyard than Wan Tiancang smiled bitterly, "Brother Li, I am afraid this demon's cultivation is not low. I fear I will be unable to help."

Li Xiangping let out a hearty laugh and spoke frankly, "Brother Wan need not talk in circles with me. Your Wan family wishes to protect the spirit rice, and my Li family wishes to protect Lichuankou. Our two families are locusts tied to the same rope!"

Wan Tiancang's heart lurched, silently crying out in misery. If this demon were not in Lichuankou, he would not care who it ate! He could have just guarded his spirit rice and laughed aloud as the Li family suffered a setback, but now he was forced to lend a hand.

Stowing away his petty thoughts, Wan Tiancang pondered for a few breaths. 

He retrieved paper and brush from the room and, while sitting down to write, explained, "Under the current circumstances, the safest plan is for me to draft a letter calling upon my family's Patriarch to come as reinforcements. It would be best if we can resolve this matter before dawn!"

"Indeed!" Li Xiangping nodded and continued, "Handwrite three letters. I will send men to leave the village from three different directions, just in case there are any accidents on the road that prevent them from reaching the Wan family's territory."

Wan Tiancang nodded repeatedly. He wrote for about a quarter of an hour when they heard screams and cries for help rising and falling outside the courtyard, plunging the area into chaos.

Li Xiangping walked out with a dark expression to inspect. A crowd of the elderly, women, and children sat in front of the courtyard, having lit their respective bonfires. One by one, they craned their necks to stare fearfully into the distance, quietly weeping or gasping in alarm.

Not far away, the village guards had already converged around the outermost group of men, nervously clutching their long clubs and door panels as they stared timidly at the neighboring woods.

"It is a wolf from the mountains."

Standing beside Li Xiangping with brush in hand, Wan Tiancang gazed into the distance at the pair of eerie green eyes in the forest and spoke in a low voice.

A giant wolf, as massive as a yellow ox[^1] and draped in a coat of silver-gray fur, was striding out of the forest with its ghostly green pupils fixed ahead. Its ash-silver snout was long and sharp, its maw wide, with upright ears and a drooping tail, forcing the village guards to retreat step after step.

Lifting its head above the crowd, that pair of eyes stared coldly at Li Xiangping standing on the stone steps of the courtyard gate. Its sharp, elongated snout curled slightly upwards, as if it were chuckling.

"This beast..." Wan Tiancang began to sweat profusely. He silently wiped away a cold sweat and continued, "Looking at that intelligent expression, it has at least already swallowed the pure essence of the sun and moon and condensed the Jade Capital Wheel."

Facing this demon directly, Li Xiangping also felt a chill run down his spine. Gazing at the giant wolf's slightly upturned snout and staring into its cold, ghostly green pupils, he actually tasted a hint of disdain.

Wan Tiancang suddenly lifted his head, quietly looking at Li Xiangping. His lips moved, and he spoke with some difficulty, "If my family's Patriarch is no match for this demon..."

"A demon of the Nascent Spirit Wheel?" Li Xiangping narrowed his eyes and replied deeply, "Prepare for the worst in all matters. If this demon's cultivation surpasses the Jade Capital Wheel, we will lure it to the southeast, bypass Lijing, and scale Mount Lijing. Our Li family has an array formation there. Your Patriarch can use the terrain to slay this demon."

"So be it, so be it!"

Wan Tiancang carefully amended the three letters and handed them to Chen Erniu.

Watching him order men to deliver the letters, Wan Tiancang shook his head with a bitter smile and said, "That beast has cultivated the Jade Capital Wheel and its spiritual intellect is open. It must have eaten many people and has already set its sights on the two of us. Cultivators are a great tonic for it; we have nowhere left to run."

"There is no need to panic." Li Xiangping waved his hand, his gaze locked tightly onto the demon's eyes.

The corners of his mouth curled upwards slightly, mirroring the wicked wolf, and similarly holding a trace of a smile, he softly said, "When I was young, I saw my father hunt wolves. These beasts are the most cautious of all. Now that it has cultivated the Jade Capital Wheel, it will undoubtedly be boundlessly cautious and cherish its own life. It is actually a good thing that it has targeted us. Just listen to my commands."

"Chen Erniu!"

"This subordinate is here!"

Chen Erniu quickly leaned in, listening carefully.

"Have the village guards retreat step by step and surround us. We will slowly withdraw to the south together. The old, weak, women, and children must remain exactly where they are and not move. That demon is certain to pursue us."

"The village guards on the perimeter are to discard their door panels entirely and hold their torches facing outwards. The moment that demon approaches, they must roar, shout, scold, or curse; they must absolutely not show the slightest hint of fear! If the demon lunges, they must sweep their long clubs bound with sabers horizontally and strike the ground. Wolf legs are thin and long; this demon will surely value them immensely."

Drawing a polished, gleaming arrow from his quiver, Li Xiangping formed a dharma technique with his fingers, and a faint golden light surfaced upon the arrowhead.

Nocking the arrow onto the bowstring, Li Xiangping commanded coldly, "Go!"

[^1]: In Chinese literature, a "yellow ox" (黄牛) refers to traditional domestic cattle. To understand the scale of this demon wolf, an adult yellow ox typically stands about 1.1 to 1.3 meters (3.6 to 4.2 feet) at the shoulder and weighs between 300 and 500 kilograms (660 to 1,100 pounds).
