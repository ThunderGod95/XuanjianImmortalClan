# Chapter 38: Imparting the Dharma

"I secretly tested your root bone a few months ago."

Li Xiangping paused for a few breaths. He turned to look down at Li Xuanxuan, whose face was brimming with excitement and anticipation, and continued, "There is no spiritual aperture in your qi sea."

Li Xuanxuan felt as though he had been struck by lightning from a clear sky. His mind went entirely blank. A surge of sorrow welled up in his heart; his eyes reddened, and his lips pressed tightly together in disbelief.

He lacked a deep understanding of just how rare spiritual apertures were in the world. He only knew that, although the number of cultivators in the entire village could be counted on one hand, two of his family elders were cultivators. Even the collateral branch had produced Li Qiuyang. Therefore, he believed that no matter how poor his innate talent was, he ought to have a spiritual aperture.

Now, hearing that he possessed no spiritual aperture and was destined to remain a mortal for the rest of his life, his heart instantly darkened, and his eyes gradually grew damp with tears.

"This mirror is a treasure I picked up while catching fish in the river as a child, " Li Xiangping said, gesturing to the grayish-blue mirror resting on the stone platform.

He looked at Li Xuanxuan's expression and gently patted his head, chuckling softly. "Do you know what wondrous uses it possesses?"

Holding back his tears, Li Xuanxuan answered earnestly, "Xuanxuan does not know."

"Its wondrous use is allowing those without a spiritual aperture to cultivate."

Li Xuanxuan froze upon hearing these words, staring blankly at Li Xiangping's face. A brilliant, sun-like hope suddenly flared in his chest, and he could not help but smile through his tears, choking out, "Third Uncle is deliberately teasing me."

However, observing Li Xiangping's ambiguous half-smile, Li Xuanxuan pondered carefully. A sudden chill ran down his spine, and he involuntarily cried out, "Could it be..."

"Indeed," Li Xiangping nodded, his expression turning solemn. "Your father has no spiritual aperture. Your Second Uncle and Third Uncle also have no spiritual apertures. Even your Fourth Uncle, Li Chejing, who cultivates in the immortal sect, lacks a spiritual aperture."

"The rise of our Li family relies entirely on this mirror. If not for this mirror, you and I would be nothing more than lowly commoners farming the fields!"

Li Xuanxuan was so struck by this revelation that he was left speechless. The world seemed to spin around him as he said hoarsely, "Simply by picking up a mirror from the river, our family has shed its mortal roots to become immortals?"

"Exactly! This mirror can refine lunar essence and bestow upon people a Profound Pearl Talisman Seed. It is incredibly miraculous. If this secret were to leak, our Li family would certainly be reduced to ash!"

Li Xiangping offered this warning, pausing slightly as he took in Li Xuanxuan's shocked expression.

Li Xuanxuan, however, muttered to himself, his voice rapid with dawning realization. "So that is how it is! So that is how it is! No wonder Grandfather always remained silent when I asked him about Foundation Establishment all day long. Our family having three spiritual apertures in one generation is not because Grandfather reached Foundation Establishment, but because of this treasure!"

"Earlier, Third Uncle stopped Li Qiuyang from touching me to conceal the secret that I lack a spiritual aperture. And the reason our family immediately killed the people of the Liu family who secretly ascended the mountain was not because Third Uncle is ruthless, but because Third Uncle is afraid..."

As Li Xuanxuan continued his train of thought, a cold sweat broke out across his back.

He cupped his hands toward Li Xiangping and said, "People all consider Third Uncle to be ruthless and paranoid, but they have misunderstood you! With our Li family guarding such a treasure, how could one not be overly cautious?"

Li Xiangping was also stunned. He stared blankly at the Li Xuanxuan before him, feeling that this child was completely different from the one in his memories.

He could not help but cast a few extra glances at him before gently asking, "Then why did I tell Li Qiuyang that you were about to reach the Profound Scenery Wheel?"

Li Xuanxuan stroked his chin, pondering for a few breaths before replying, "It must be because this mirror yields twice the result with half the effort in forging the Profound Scenery Wheel! Third Uncle was terrified that Li Qiuyang would grow suspicious, so you lied and claimed I had begun cultivating several months ago."

"Good!" Li Xiangping nodded in satisfaction and patted his shoulder. "You truly are a child of my Li family. Following behind your grandfather every day, you have indeed learned quite a lot!"

With that said, he bent down and tapped lightly on the base of the stone platform, retrieving a wooden slip from the side. Placing it into Li Xuanxuan's hands and watching as the boy received it respectfully, he then explained in a low voice.

"This secret method is called the ⟨Reception Method⟩. It is a wondrous technique used to receive the Profound Pearl Talisman Seed. You will study it carefully in this courtyard for the next month, and on the Winter Solstice, you will be bestowed with the Talisman Seed. You will stay in the side chamber next door, and I will have someone deliver your meals."

"Yes."

Li Xuanxuan nodded heavily. Watching Li Xiangping close the door and his footsteps gradually fade away, he lowered his head and began reading attentively.

"...Father is in good health, and Second Brother has successfully cultivated the Heavenly Circuit Wheel. Enclosed with this letter are three Serpent Essence Pills. We hope Jing'er will cultivate diligently and soon attain Qi Refinement. All is well at home, do not worry!"

Li Chejing carefully put away the letter in his hands and wiped the tears from the corners of his eyes. Placing the three bottles of Serpent Essence Pills neatly onto the shelf, he turned to Xiao Yuansi and expressed his gratitude, "Many thanks to you, Senior Brother! My family has received much of your kindness, and Chejing is endlessly grateful."

"Think nothing of it!" Xiao Yuansi shook his head and replied, "Fellow disciples are as close as family. There is no need for such politeness."

Li Chejing nodded slightly and spoke softly, "With these Serpent Essence Pills, combined with the Clear Spirit Powder bestowed by Master, I am ninety percent certain of condensing the Jade Capital Wheel."

Pondering for a few moments, Li Chejing asked gently, "Master once said that to transition from Fetal Breathing to Qi Refinement, one must first swallow a mouthful of the spiritual qi of heaven and earth. I wonder, what is the key to this?"

Xiao Yuansi picked up his teacup and took a sip to moisten his throat, before explaining, "For Qi Refinement, one must first absorb a mouthful of the spiritual qi of heaven and earth. This spiritual qi could be the Lesser Clear Spiritual Qi refined from the mountain's ambient energy, or the Earth Vein Malevolent Qi refined from the earth vein's malevolent aura, or the Morning Glow Rainbow Qi gathered from dawn mist..."

"In short, it is best if this breath of energy is compatible with one's cultivation art. Only then can it be pure and vigorous, forging a solid foundation for one's dharma power. Ninety-nine percent of the rogue cultivators outside the mountains use the Lesser Clear Spiritual Qi refined from mountain qi. It is compatible with all sorts of cultivation arts, possessing neither distinct advantages nor obvious flaws."

"Oh?"

Li Chejing felt a burst of curiosity upon hearing this, hastily asking, "What kind of Qi Refinement art would be best to transition to from my family's cultivation method? And what spiritual qi should I use?"

"Although I do not know your family's cultivation art, observing that your dharma power is clear and bright, there are quite a few Qi Refinement dharma techniques within the sect suitable for your transition."

Xiao Yuansi smiled. Setting down his teacup, he continued under Li Chejing's curious gaze, "The first is called the ⟨Primordial Purity Rain-Controlling Art⟩. It is the most renowned supreme art of our Azure Pond Sect. It utilizes the Rainmist qi refined from the rain and fog of spiritual mountains. It excels in riding clouds and mist, manipulating rain, and using softness to overcome adversaries[^1]."

"The second is the ⟨Mountain Yin Forest Mist Art⟩. This technique utilizes the Yin qi found within mountains[^2]. Very few in the sect have successfully cultivated it."

"The third, and most challenging, is called the ⟨Moon Lake Autumn Reflection Art⟩. Ever since the third Peak Master of Moon Lake Peak successfully cultivated it, no one else has been able to master it. Perhaps there is some crucial secret to it, but that person perished over three hundred years ago, so there is nowhere left to ask..."

Knowing that his own dharma power was deeply connected to lunar essence, Li Chejing felt an immediate surge of joy upon hearing the name. Yet, upon hearing Xiao Yuansi say that no one could master it, he froze and hastily asked, "Why is that?"

[^1]: This concept is deeply rooted in classical Daoist philosophy, primarily derived from Laozi's _Daodejing_ (Tao Te Ching).
    
    A central tenet of Daoism is _yǐ róu kè gāng_ (以柔克刚), meaning "using softness to overcome hardness."
    
    Laozi frequently uses water as the ultimate metaphor for the Dao, noting that while water is the softest and most yielding substance in the world, it can erode the hardest rocks over time.
    
    The martial application of this philosophu focuses on using an opponent's momentum against them.
[^2]: In Daoist cosmology, the universe is composed of Yin and Yang energies. Yang represents heat, light, the sun, masculinity, and activity. Yin qi represents cold, darkness, moisture, the moon, femininity, and stillness. It is an energy of accumulation, rest, and shadow.

    In traditional Chinese geography and Feng Shui, the term "Mountain Yin" (山阴 - Shanyin) has a very specific meaning: the northern face or slope of a mountain. Because China is in the Northern Hemisphere, the north side of a mountain receives little to no direct sunlight. Consequently, this side is perpetually cooler, darker, and more prone to retaining moisture and dense vegetation. These exact environmental conditions make the northern slopes natural gathering points for Yin qi.
    
    The origin of the ⟨Mountain Yin Forest Mist Art⟩ (山阴林雾诀) can be deduced directly from its name. "Shanyin" refers to the northern face of the mountain, and "Linwu" translates to forest mist. Therefore, the technique was created by observing and harnessing the specific environmental phenomena of the northern slopes—specifically, the cold, damp, and dense Yin-aspected mist that accumulates in the sunless forests of spiritual mountains. Practitioners of this art draw this specific geographical Yin energy into their bodies to cultivate their dharma power.
