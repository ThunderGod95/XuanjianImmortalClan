# Chapter 23: Spiritual Aperture

Li Tongya formed a hand seal, watching the fine, dense spirit rain drift down onto the pitch-black soil.

His face was pale as he pondered, _This Spirit Rain Art from the spirit plant cultivation method truly drains a massive amount of energy. Even after condensing the Bearing Brightness Wheel, I can barely unleash it once. These spirit plants still require daily care; we are truly in desperate need of manpower._

Li Xiangping and Li Tongya had measured the spirit fields across the villages, finding them all pitifully scarce. The spirit fields in Lijing were fragmented; fortunately, the largest plot could barely accommodate ten White Essence Fruits.

Thus, Li Tongya dispatched men to build a small courtyard at the foot of the mountain, upon a plot that was originally wasteland. He usually stayed here to cultivate his arts and tend the fields. Naturally, people delivered his daily necessities, making for a rather pleasant existence.

Li Xiangping, on the other hand, selected a spirit field in Jingyang, sowing spirit rice there. This drew the people of Jingyang to bow and worship outside his courtyard day after day, a sight he found quite amusing.

Because he had not yet entered the Heavenly Circuit Wheel, Li Tongya closed his eyes and regulated his breathing for nearly half an hour before his dharma power was fully restored. Checking the hour, he realized Li Yesheng and his men had already been waiting outside the door for quite some time.

Hearing the wooden door creak open, Li Yesheng hurriedly bowed and greeted him, "The children from all the villages have gathered. Brother Tongya, shall we go now?"

"Mhm."

Li Tongya gave a low response. Amidst the attentive smiles of Li Yesheng and the rugged men behind him, he slowly walked toward the head of the village.

---

Beneath the lush, leafy expanse of the great locust tree[^1] at the edge of the village, the representatives sent by the various villages had all arrived. The stewards dispatched by the Li family led the way, accompanied by several prominent villagers, with throngs of children of all ages following behind.

The several stewards appeared rather relaxed, standing in pairs beneath the locust tree and conversing in hushed tones. The villagers from outside settlements, however, seemed anxious and constrained; not daring to speak, they stood stiffly in place. Infected by this atmosphere, the children sat obediently on the ground, one by one.

Chen Erniu had been riding high on the spring breeze of success lately, so overjoyed that sleep eluded him at night. For countless generations, his ancestors had crawled and toiled in the dirt, and now, finally, he was living a life worthy of a man.

Upon learning he had been assigned to manage Lichuankou, Chen Erniu had fiercely knocked his head against the ground nine times before the Li family courtyard.

Weeping bitterly before the old master of the Li family, he made a fierce vow, "Blessed by the old master's trust, Erniu will absolutely beat those village men of Lichuankou into total submission."

He had received the Li family's relief when he fled from disaster. He owed them for taking a wife and buying land, and it was the Li family who paid for his wife's medical treatment when she fell ill. This boundless grace had already bound Chen Erniu firmly to the Li family's war chariot[^2].

Furthermore, as long as overwhelming benefits were placed before his eyes, he, Chen Erniu, would even dare to eat dog filth!

It was just that Chen Erniu and Xu Wenshan had never seen eye to eye since their days as tenant farmers, leading to frequent friction between their two villages. Only Ren Ping'an held a transcendent position among the three villages, managing his own village affairs in solitude every day.

"Steward Chen..."

An anxious call rang in his ears. Chen Erniu turned his head and saw it was Chen Changhu, a fellow villager who had come from Lichuankou.

The Chen clan made up the majority in Lichuankou. This Chen Changhu was the eldest within the clan and held considerable prestige in the township, hence everyone had recommended him to lead the group.

This old man, usually so composed and highly respected, now gazed toward the village entrance with a face full of trepidation, inquiring in a low voice, "Will this immortal of the Li family truly accept the scions of my Chen clan?"

"They said one must have immortal destiny! One must have that blasted aperture! Children between seven and thirteen, as long as your Chen clan's children possess a spiritual aperture, the Li family will take them all!" Chen Erniu assured.

Watching Chen Changhu nod repeatedly, Chen Erniu curled his lip, though his heart brimmed with endless envy. He secretly cursed the fact that his own two children were either too old, having missed the opportunity, or too young to even attend the testing.

"The immortal is here!"

Seeing Li Tongya appear at the village entrance, Chen Erniu hurriedly smiled and went to greet him, thinking enviously in his heart, _I wonder which of these children from the villages will attain immortal destiny today._

Li Tongya offered a light bow with cupped hands, smiled at Chen Erniu, and turned to Li Yesheng, "Let us begin."

A wooden platform had long been erected beside the locust tree. Li Tongya sat upon the wooden stage, and the people below began to call out the names.

"Lichuankou! Chen Lifu!"

From below, members of the Chen clan brought up a trembling child.

Li Tongya rested his hand upon the boy's shoulder and circulated his dharma power once through the child's body.

Waving his hand, he said, "Step down."

Hearing these words, the two dared not utter a sound and hurriedly retreated. A flash of disappointment crossed Chen Changhu's eyes below.

"Next!"

---

Before long, all the people from Lichuankou had been dismissed. Chen Changhu's face was ashen as he looked toward Chen Erniu with what seemed like a pleading gaze.

Chen Erniu rolled his eyes and spread his hands, indicating there was nothing he could do to help.

The late autumn weather was crisp and cold. Even at high noon, with so many people gathered, it remained cool beneath the tree. Yet, the villagers from all the settlements were drenched in sweat. Seeing that Lichuankou had failed to produce a single shred of immortal destiny, their hearts pounded with secret dread.

Li Tongya, however, had long prepared himself mentally for this scene. Those possessing a spiritual aperture were one in a thousand. If the children from all these villages could produce even one, it would be enough to recoup their efforts; two would be an absolute gain.

"Jingyang! Liu Rouxuan!"

As soon as the voice fell, a girl of about thirteen or fourteen stepped forward. Her brows and eyes were curved and delicate, her eyes bright as she timidly bit her lip.

Fourteen years old was right at the upper limit of the age requirement. Li Tongya shook his head helplessly, pretending not to notice, and rested his left hand on the girl's shoulder to circulate his dharma power.

After his power drifted through the girl's meridians for a cycle, Li Tongya let out a soft exclamation of surprise. Below, the members of the Liu clan from Jingyang could not help but tense up.

He perceived a ceaseless surge of flowing qi within the girl's Qihai aperture, pulsing steadily around a transparent qi cavity, as if it were breathing on its own.

"Not bad." Li Tongya opened his eyes, smiled at the girl, and said gently, "Stand behind me."

Liu Rouxuan stared blankly into Li Tongya's eyes, seemingly unable to process this heaven-shaking fortune. Only after a few breaths did realization dawn upon her, and she took her place behind Li Tongya with radiant joy.

The Jingyang villagers below immediately broke into a clamor. Everyone wore an expression of delight, only restraining their cheers out of deference to Li Tongya. Lichuankou was engulfed in a deathly gloom, while the remaining two villages grew even more apprehensive.

Before long, both Jingyang and Lidaokou finished their testing. As expected, there were no others who possessed a spiritual aperture.

"Lijing!"

Li Tongya cast his gaze low, sweeping over the children below. Naturally, Li Xuanxuan was not among them; the child was only three years old, his body underdeveloped. He would have to reach at least seven before they could verify if he possessed a spiritual aperture.

Finally, it was the turn of the children from their own village. Li Yesheng raised an eyebrow and began to call the names.

_If Brother Tongya hadn't already tested me earlier, I'd truly be itching to go up there and try!_

Li Yesheng thought bitterly as he loudly called out the names.

"Ye Qiuyang!"

Below, his father, Ye Changfu, suddenly tensed up, his legs turning to jelly. His eyes were fixed entirely on the eight or nine-year-old child on the stage, his heart constantly comforting itself, _My Ye clan is a branch of the Li family after all. We must carry some measure of immortal destiny within us..._

Watching Li Tongya place his hand upon Ye Qiuyang's shoulder, Ye Changfu closed his eyes and simply plopped his bottom onto the sandy ground, listening in a daze.

[^1]: Despite the English name, this has absolutely no connection to the swarming grasshopper-like insects.

    It refers to a type of shade tree (often the Chinese Scholar Tree or Japanese Pagoda Tree) that is ubiquitous in northern Chinese villages. 
    
    Because of their massive size and longevity, they traditionally serve as the central gathering point, marketplace, and communal hub for rural settlements.
[^2]: It is a figure of speech meaning to be inextricably bound to a specific faction, cause, or individual's destiny.
