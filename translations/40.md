# Chapter 40: Demon

Chen Erniu leapt from his bed, snatched the clothes by his bedside, and hastily threw them on.

Dragging his son, he dashed toward the door, hurriedly asking, "What in the hell is going on?"

The eldest son of the Chen family, Chen Sanshui, was anxiously reaching for the long saber on the wall, crying out, "Take the saber, Father, take the saber! We can talk on the way!"

Chen Erniu took the long saber, fastened it around his waist, and ran toward the edge of the village with great strides.

"A village guard got up in the night to patrol the fields and heard a rustling sound, thinking someone was out there. He assumed a couple was having a tryst in the fields and quickly ducked down to peek. Instead, he saw a bloody corpse with the back of its head entirely missing. He soiled himself in terror on the spot."

Chen Sanshui said this while hurriedly keeping pace with Chen Erniu.

"How did he die?"

"The back of his head was smashed open and his brains were dug out. The corpse looks ghastly."

Hearing this, Chen Erniu abruptly halted his steps and asked in astonishment, "Has anyone been sent to notify the main family?"

"Not yet..." Chen Sanshui replied, somewhat evasively.

"Fool! Do you think a matter like this can be hidden?!" Chen Erniu immediately flew into a rage, shouting with furious disappointment.

Seeing his father's furious glare, Chen Sanshui hastily explained, "I had already discussed it with that man and the few families by the fields... I told them not to spread the word for now. This is the first murder in Lichuankou in years, and I feared Xu Wenshan might use it against you, Father!"

"Fool! The man had his brains dug out!"

"This..."

Seeing the shock and rage on his father's face, Chen Sanshui hesitated for a moment as realization began to dawn on him.

"Would an ordinary murderer dig out someone's brains?! Furthermore, my feud with Xu Wenshan is merely a show for others; we are both perfectly content with the arrangement. Would Xu Wenshan really kick me while I am down? If he destroys me, he would needlessly offend the over one hundred households here in Lichuankou!"

"You brothers are all as stupid as pigs! Xu Wenshan is younger than I am. If I am gone one day, what will you use to contend with others?!"

"Father..."

This sharp rebuke left Chen Sanshui overcome with shame. He lowered his head and stammered, saying no more.

Chen Erniu's face darkened, his heart exceptionally heavy with frustration, and he said coldly, "I fear a demon is wreaking havoc. Go and notify the main family yourself."

Chen Sanshui nodded vigorously and hurriedly departed toward Lijing.

Watching his eldest son retreat into the distance, Chen Erniu's face finally revealed a trace of terror and sorrow as he softly said, "I do not know the depth of this demon's power. If Sanshui keeps his wits about him, he might still survive to preserve our Chen family lineage."

Chen Erniu had personally survived that terrifying drought in Lichuankou. Still a young child at the time, he had been playing in front of his door when he watched a giant bird, wreathed in crimson flames, descend upon Lichuankou.

It swallowed several village guards as if pecking at small insects before taking flight, leaving Lichuankou with steaming, scorching fields for three full months.

The surviving villagers ate absolutely everything edible around Lichuankou. They scooped water from the Meichi River and poured it into the fields, only for it to completely vanish. Chen Erniu's father carried load after load of water, watering the land for nine whole days, before taking his own life while embracing his starved wife.

Leaving behind his parents' corpses, Chen Erniu gritted his teeth and, fighting back tears, fled to Lijing. He knocked his head against the ground until it bled, finally earning a place in the Li family.

At that time, every household in Lijing was packed with fellow villagers who had fled there days prior. Some attempted to attack the wealthy households, only to be beaten to death by tenant farmers who had been their neighbors just days before. The vast majority had no choice but to plunge headlong into the Great Li Mountains.

Three months later, the people slowly returned. No one dared to speak of that day, nor did anyone dare to speak of how the over one hundred people who remained in the village had survived. In silence, the people buried the human bones that had been licked entirely clean. Of the original nearly three hundred households and fourteen hundred people in Lichuankou, barely more than three hundred souls remained.

Seeing the flickering torchlight before him, Chen Erniu was jolently awakened from his memories. He pushed aside his second son, Chen Qiushui, who had come to meet him, and raised his torch.

Looking at the bloody corpse collapsed on the ground with an empty skull, tears were already streaming down his face as he gnashed his teeth and asked, "Who is this?"

Seeing the tears on his father's face, Chen Qiushui felt deeply uneasy and answered in a low voice, "Old Ye from the edge of the village."

"Go rouse the entire village. Light the bonfires and take up your sabers and clubs."

Chen Erniu instructed quietly. Just then, a village guard came rushing over from the village entrance.

Seeing him, the guard spoke up, "Manager Chen, Manager Chen! The main family has sent word to ask: Is there any disturbance in Lichuankou?"

Chen Erniu blanked for a moment, great shock and countless suspicions rising in his heart.

He thought to himself, _There is no way a message could travel from Lichuankou to Lijing so quickly! Even if an informant in the village reported it, a round trip could not be this fast. The main household must have known the moment this villager died. They must have some method to deal with that demon._

His tightly knit brows instantly relaxed. Feeling considerably relieved, Chen Erniu answered loudly, "We fear a demon is wreaking havoc in the village! Someone has already been sent to inform the main family!"

---

Li Xiangping silently watched the grayish-blue mirror on the stand. Its fragmented surface seemed slightly more restored compared to when he had found it as a child, yet it still looked as if it could shatter at any moment. A faint, silvery-white glow emanated from the mirror's face, appearing quite dreamlike.

"How long has that man been gone?"

"He has been gone for a quarter of an hour," Li Tongya replied, looking out the window with some anxiety.

He set down the wooden slip in his hand and suggested, "We should head out. If those people cannot find us, they might break the rules and come into this back courtyard."

Li Xiangping glanced at Li Xuanxuan, who was sitting cross-legged, and said in a deep voice, "Let us go!"

Scarcely had they stepped out of the back courtyard when they saw Li Yesheng pacing in circles around the main courtyard like an ant on a hot pan.

Upon seeing Li Tongya and Li Xiangping, Li Yesheng's eyes immediately lit up, and he hastily said, "Lichuankou has sent someone! They said someone's brains were dug out!"

"Brains dug out?" Li Xiangping paused, looking at Li Tongya as he spoke, "I fear it is a demon."

"Indeed." Li Tongya nodded gravely. He pondered for a few breaths before saying in a deep voice, "The demon dug out the brains and then retreated. This means it still fears humans, and its cultivation cannot be too high. It must still be in the Fetal Breathing stage. If it were at the Qi Refinement stage, it could have easily slaughtered the entire village and departed upon the clouds."

"I will go take a look!" Li Xiangping pondered for a few breaths and furrowed his brow slightly. He turned to Li Yesheng and commanded, "Gather the able-bodied men in the village and bring weapons."

"Understood!" Li Yesheng gave a few rapid affirmations and hurried off. 

Once Li Yesheng was far away, Li Tongya finally frowned and said, "We do not know the depths of this demon's power. We should not go."

"If we do not go today, the prestige our Li family has painstakingly built among these villages will be completely ruined. I have no choice but to go!"

"Second Brother, you stand guard on the mountain. I will go ahead to investigate. You know my temper; I will not take unnecessary risks."

"Even if something happens, our Li family will at least still have you. At worst, we abandon Lichuankou and seek aid from the sect."

With that, Li Xiangping nodded to Li Tongya, took up his bow and arrows, donned his yellowish-brown rattan armor, and hurriedly descended the mountain.
