# Chapter 7: Techniques

Early morning.

The dazzling dawn crest over the Great Li Mountains, caressing the golden Moongaze Lake. The wind swept across the endless, overlapping reed marshes, carrying with it the playful laughter of children on the Meichi River.

Chen Erniu sat on the ridge between the fields, staring at the crops with deep worry, his mind on his bedridden wife.

Chen Erniu was born in Lichuankou, east of the Great Li Mountains. Over twenty years ago, Lichuankou suffered an inexplicable, severe drought out of nowhere. Not a single drop of rain fell, and the earth aggressively puffed out white smoke, terrifying the villagers into fleeing in all directions.

He was only a half-grown boy back then, stumbling his way to Lijing Village in a daze. After his bitter begging, the farmer Li Genshui took him in, and the following year, even rented him a mu of land so he could survive.

When he grew older, he rented another mu of paddy field, built a dirt house, married Li Genshui's daughter, and successfully put down his roots in the village with his head held high.

But his wife had suddenly fallen ill. Fortunately, he had gathered some savings over the years. He carried her to the village entrance for Teacher Han to examine—it was merely a minor illness, requiring only a few doses of medicine. He settled his wife at the teacher's place, but his heart remained suspended in his throat. A single illness could impoverish three generations!

"Uncle-in-law!" Chen Erniu was lost in thought when a hearty voice startled him awake.

He saw a handsome young man walking into the courtyard area, a warm smile on his face, waving in greeting.

"Ah, Changhu! I cannot accept such a title, I cannot!"

Chen Erniu bowed repeatedly. He had merely married a daughter born of a concubine from the Li family, and he was their tenant farmer to boot. He truly could not bear such a respectful address from the eldest son.

Li Genshui had been shrewd and capable, but exceptionally unfaithful. Marrying a wife was not enough; he had also picked up two women from the road to be his concubines. His wife had borne him two sons, while the concubines gave birth to three sons and four daughters.

Back when Li Genshui was old and near death, his only legitimate young son who remained by his side died suddenly. The strong and youthful sons of the concubines eyed the family's ten-odd mu of land like hungry tigers.

It was then that Li Mutian, who had run away from home, returned with a blade and men in tow. This brutal veteran had killed the treacherous steward with a single slash. The wealthy family that had poisoned his younger brother in preparation for annexing their land was slaughtered entirely—not even a dog was spared.

Li Mutian and his two brothers in arms, carrying blood-dripping blades, used a pushcart to drag the corpses. The bloody trail stretched from the village's end to its entrance. Every household in the village barred their doors, everyone fearing for their lives. Li Mutian's half-brothers were terrified out of their wits, assuming that since the rightful heir had returned, they would certainly have no share of the fields.

Yet, surprisingly, Li Mutian called the brothers together. He declared that legitimate and illegitimate sons shared the same roots and branches, dividing the wealthy family's confiscated land by giving each brother two mu, and giving his own comrades-in-arms four mu each. The brothers were so grateful that they repeatedly hailed him as the eldest brother of the main branch, thus solidifying his proper status.

Looking at Li Changhu's gentle smile, Chen Erniu somehow overlapped it with Li Mutian's indifferent, blood-spattered face.

Despite the blazing sun overhead, he shuddered and asked respectfully, "What brings you here, Changhu?"

"Ah, you are too polite, Uncle." Li Changhu smiled and said softly, "We heard that aunt is ill. My father figured you would not have the time to cook, so he specifically sent me to invite you and Little Ze to our home to taste my mother's cooking."

"This... I am too embarrassed." Chen Erniu forced a smile, caught in a dilemma.

"Then it is settled! Mother has already prepared the food." Li Changhu patted Chen Erniu's shoulder, bid his farewell, and left.

"Alright." Chen Erniu smiled, shaking his head, and called into the house, "Little Ze, tidy up. We are going to your uncle's house for dinner tonight."

---

The Li family had finished renovating their residence the year before last, expanding the grounds several times over. The entire compound faced south in a rectangular layout. The front courtyard was paved with stone bricks and featured stone locks[^1], making it look like a place for martial arts practice. Seeing the considerable weight of the stone lock, Chen Erniu could not help but click his tongue in praise.

"Could it be that the Li family also possesses a martial arts method? What a magnificent stone lock."

Stepping onto the stone bricks, he entered the main courtyard. A pond had been built in the center, stocked with a few black carp. To the left and right of the main hall, which was enclosed by bluestone, were the side wings for Li Changhu and Li Tongya. The bases of the rooms, the verandas, the main gate, and the secondary gate were laid with stone blocks or steps, giving the place an extremely grand appearance.

Li Tongya greeted them and also entered the main courtyard. He was already eighteen this year but had not yet taken a wife. Li Changhu, however, had married the second daughter of the Ren family; they had held a grand wedding for her just a short time ago.

Chen Erniu and the Li family members ate dinner and sat in the courtyard, chatting idly. Suddenly, Li Chejing hurried into the front courtyard, leaned close to Li Mutian's ear, and whispered a few words.

Li Chejing was only nine years old but possessed a handsome appearance and a clever, intelligent demeanor, making him especially beloved by his relatives and brothers.

Li Mutian was leisurely watching the younger generation chat when he heard Li Chejing whisper in his ear, "Father, that... mirror lit up!"

Maintaining his composure, he patted his legs and addressed the crowd, "I am getting old and can sit no longer. I shall go rest; you all continue your chat."

With that, he turned and headed to the back courtyard. Everyone quickly voiced their assent, and Chen Erniu bowed, preparing to take his leave.

Several wing rooms stood in the rear hall. Li Mutian entered the ancestral hall, which sat in the very center and was the largest room. Inside, spirit tablets stood at the front, flanked by offerings of fruit, venerating six generations of ancestors dug up from the village records.

Li Mutian pushed against the wall, revealing a hidden compartment behind it.

The compartment had a skylight, allowing moonlight to spill onto a stone altar built from bluestone.

Atop the altar rested a grayish-blue bronze mirror, which was indeed radiating a bright, water-like white light.

"Three years... Jing'er, go fetch your brothers."

Li Mutian frowned as he looked at the grayish-blue bronze mirror.

"Yes." Li Chejing nodded vigorously and went out to find his brothers.

---

When Lu Jiangxian awoke, his mind was heavily stuffed with newfound knowledge. He rested for the time it took an incense stick to burn before calming his heart to read the information on the jade.

⟨Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture⟩!

This technique primarily taught how to manipulate the spiritual energy of heaven and earth through the human body's apertures, drawing upon the Supreme Yin lunar essence.

Ultimately, by cultivating the Six Wheels of Fetal Breathing, one could use these six wheels to extend their lifespan, nourish their nature, and cast various arts. Once the six wheels were nurtured until they were like a full moon, one could enter the Qi Condensation stage.

Attached to it were several minor arts, such as the ⟨Golden Light Art⟩, ⟨Cleanse Clothing Art⟩, ⟨Water Repelling Method⟩, ⟨Evil Warding Art⟩, and ⟨Mind Network Art⟩. As long as one cultivated the six wheels, they could cast them by channeling spiritual energy according to the technique.

At the same time, the text mentioned that the six stages of immortal cultivation were divided into Fetal Breathing, Qi Condensation, Foundation Establishment, Purple Mansion, Golden Core, and Nascent Soul.

The text recorded on this jade was precisely the ⟨Fetal Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Method⟩ of the Yue State's Moonlight Origin Mansion, distributed for its fetal breathing disciples to study and cultivate.

After devouring the jade, Lu Jiangxian felt as though he had invisibly comprehended many things. Not only had his divine sense expanded to a range of twenty zhang[^2], but his lunar essence capacity had also multiplied several dozen times over.

As long as he had enough lunar essence, the minor arts recorded in the ⟨Supreme Yin Breathing Wheel-Nurturing Scripture⟩ could also be cast within one zhang of the mirror's body.

Most importantly, an art surfaced within the mirror's own memory, called:

⟨Profound Pearl Spirit-Sacrificing Art⟩!
